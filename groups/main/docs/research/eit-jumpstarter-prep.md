## 1. Program Overview

### What Is EIT Jumpstarter?

EIT Jumpstarter is a pan-European pre-acceleration program run by the **European Institute of Innovation & Technology (EIT)** — an EU body created to drive innovation and entrepreneurship across Europe.

The program targets early-stage startups with innovative ideas in key sectors including:
- Climate and sustainability (EIT Climate-KIC)
- Food and agriculture (EIT Food)
- Digital transformation (EIT Digital)
- Urban mobility (EIT Urban Mobility)
- Health (EIT Health)
- Manufacturing and raw materials (EIT Manufacturing / EIT RawMaterials)

**Key distinguishing feature**: EIT Jumpstarter does NOT require an existing company. It's designed for ideas and very early-stage founders — making it ideal for pre-revenue or early-revenue startups like YourWave.

### Funding & Support Package

| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Grant amount | €10,000 (equity-free grant) |
| Mentorship | 4–6 months of structured mentorship from EIT network |
| Training | Business model development, pitch coaching, go-to-market |
| Network access | EIT alumni, investors, corporate partners across EU |
| Certification | EIT-issued innovation certificate |
| Follow-on pathway | Priority access to EIT acceleration programs (€50K–€100K+ grants) |

### Program Dates (2026 Cycle)

| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| Applications open | Typically January–February 2026 |
| **Application deadline** | **May 8, 2026** |
| Selection notification | June–July 2026 |
| Program start | September–October 2026 |
| Program duration | 4–6 months |

### Eligibility Requirements

- **Stage**: Idea stage to early revenue (pre-Series A)
- **Team**: Minimum 1 founder; team preferred but solo accepted
- **Geography**: Founder or company must be based in EU/EEA country (CZ qualifies)
- **Sector fit**: Must align with one of EIT's innovation communities
- **Age of company**: Typically <3 years old at time of application
- **Previous funding**: No restriction on previous grants but must not be post-Series A

**YourWave sector fit**: EIT Food is the most relevant community — specialty coffee falls within sustainable food systems, food traceability, and consumer food innovation.

## 2. Evaluation Criteria

EIT Jumpstarter evaluates applications on five main dimensions:

### Criterion 1: Innovation & Novelty (25%)
- Is the idea new? Does it solve a problem in a genuinely different way?
- YourWave angle: Education-first DTC coffee platform with Atlas as public knowledge layer — distinct from commodity coffee subscription model

### Criterion 2: Market Potential (20%)
- Addressable market size; growth trajectory; international scalability potential
- YourWave angle: CZ specialty coffee market (~€80–120M) as entry; Central European expansion path; EU specialty coffee market growing at 8–12% CAGR

### Criterion 3: Team Capability (20%)
- Relevant skills, domain knowledge, ability to execute
- YourWave angle: Founder credibility in product/tech + genuine domain knowledge in specialty coffee; Atlas content demonstrates deep expertise

### Criterion 4: Sustainability Impact (20%)
- Environmental, social, or economic sustainability dimension
- YourWave angle: Direct trade relationships, fair price transparency, reducing waste in coffee supply chain, education reducing greenwashing in consumer coffee choices

### Criterion 5: Feasibility & Traction (15%)
- Evidence of progress: early customers, MVP, research, prototypes
- YourWave angle: Published website, Atlas with multiple articles, initial user validation, CZ market research completed

## 4. Draft Application Structure

### Section 1: Problem Statement

**Draft content for YourWave:**

> Czech consumers are increasingly interested in specialty coffee but lack accessible, trustworthy educational resources to navigate quality, origin, and sustainable sourcing. The result: informed purchasing decisions are impossible for most buyers, sustainable farms and transparent roasters are invisible in a sea of commodity marketing, and the gap between consumer curiosity and actual behavior remains wide.
>
> Globally, the specialty coffee market is growing at 8–12% CAGR, but 90% of "specialty" marketing is greenwashing — unverifiable claims with no traceability. Czech consumers, who are among Europe's most research-intensive shoppers, are poorly served by the existing information ecosystem.

**Key metrics to include**:
- CZ specialty coffee market: ~€80–120M, growing 10%+ annually
- % of Czech consumers who cannot identify coffee origin on the bag they buy: estimated 85%+
- Number of Czech specialty coffee articles in Czech language: <200 (underserved content gap)

### Section 2: Solution — YourWave Platform

**Draft content:**

> YourWave is an education-first DTC coffee platform for the Czech Republic, built around three interlocking layers:
>
> **1. The Atlas** — A public, SEO-optimized knowledge base of specialty coffee: origins, processing methods, roasters, tasting frameworks, and brewing guides. Free to access, designed to be the Czech Wikipedia of specialty coffee.
>
> **2. The Store** — A curated DTC subscription service connecting Czech consumers directly to exceptional coffee lots, with full traceability and the Atlas context embedded at every purchase decision.
>
> **3. The Community** — Content, events, and community around elevating Czech coffee culture.
>
> The key innovation: most coffee brands sell first and educate never. YourWave educates first, builds trust, and converts informed readers into loyal subscribers. This creates a structurally different customer acquisition model — content-driven, low-CAC, high-LTV.

### Section 7: Sustainability Impact Statement

**Draft content:**

> YourWave creates measurable sustainability impact across three dimensions:
>
> **1. Supply chain transparency**: Every coffee lot on YourWave includes full traceability data — farm, country, elevation, processing method, fair price paid. This makes sustainable sourcing choices accessible to consumers who currently cannot evaluate competing claims.
>
> **2. Education as demand shift**: By educating Czech consumers about quality indicators, we shift demand from commodity (often mass-deforestation-linked) to traceable specialty (smallholder farm-supporting). One informed consumer is worth 100 generic "buy sustainable" campaigns.
>
> **3. Direct roaster relationships**: YourWave's sourcing model prioritizes roasters with verifiable direct trade relationships, excluding commodity-sourced coffee regardless of price competition.
>
> **Measurable goals (12 months)**:
> - 500+ consumers educated through Atlas content
> - 100% of sourced coffee traceable to country of origin
> - 3+ roasters with verified direct trade relationships featured
> - Carbon footprint disclosure for each shipment (stretch goal)
