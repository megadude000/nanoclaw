## 2. Channel Deep Dives

### 2.1 Instagram

**CZ Audience**: 3.2M monthly active users. Coffee-interested segment estimated at 180K–250K based on hashtag reach data.

**CPA Estimate**:
- Organic: €8–15 per subscription (via DM conversions + link-in-bio)
- Paid (Instagram Ads): €18–30 per subscription (CZ CPM €3–6)

**Pros for Coffee DTC**:
- Visual-first medium perfectly suited to coffee aesthetics
- Strong Czech specialty coffee community already active
- Reels algorithm rewards niche content with outsized reach
- Low barrier to start; no ad spend needed initially
- Story features (polls, Q&A) enable two-way community building

**Cons**:
- Algorithm changes create unpredictability
- Organic reach declining for static posts (2–4% of followers)
- Requires consistent content production
- No direct purchase integration in CZ (Instagram Shopping not fully enabled for CZ DTC)

**Czech-Specific Notes**:
- Czech users are more private than Western European counterparts — DM culture is smaller
- Czech-language captions significantly boost local engagement
- Prague coffee scene is Instagram-active; Brno and other cities underpenetrated

**YourWave Fit**: ★★★★★ — Primary channel. Start here.

---

### 2.2 TikTok

**CZ Audience**: 1.8M active users, skewing 18–34. Growing rapidly — Czech brands are still early.

**CPA Estimate**:
- Organic: €6–18 per conversion (high variance based on viral potential)
- TikTok Ads CZ: €12–22 per conversion (CPM €1.50–4)

**Pros for Coffee DTC**:
- Highest organic reach potential — the algorithm shows content to non-followers by default
- "How-to" and education content performs extremely well (#LearnOnTikTok style)
- Coffee brewing videos have proven virality globally (see: WeierstrassP, James Hoffmann-style content)
- CZ TikTok coffee creators are almost nonexistent — first-mover advantage available
- Low production quality acceptable; authenticity > polish

**Cons**:
- Requires video-only format — time investment is higher than Instagram
- Czech TikTok audience younger and not yet high-converting for premium products
- Platform uncertainty (regulatory pressure in EU creates some risk)
- Harder to drive traffic to website vs. Instagram

**Czech-Specific Notes**:
- Czech TikTok is heavily entertainment-focused; educational niches are genuinely underserved
- "Kávový tutorial" style content could own a micro-niche with low competition
- Czech-language content still preferred for comments/community

**YourWave Fit**: ★★★★☆ — Secondary channel. Activate Month 2–3 once Instagram content rhythm established.

---

### 2.3 Pinterest

**CZ Audience**: ~1.1M CZ Pinterest users. Skews female, 25–45, lifestyle and home-focused.

**CPA Estimate**: €12–35 per conversion (longer attribution window)

**Pros for Coffee DTC**:
- Evergreen content — Pins have 6–12 month average lifespan vs. Instagram's 24–48 hours
- High purchase intent audience (Pinterest users actively plan purchases)
- Coffee aesthetics (flat lays, home brewing, café vibes) are native Pinterest content
- Atlas content (recipes, coffee guides) maps perfectly to Pinterest boards
- Very low competition in CZ coffee space

**Cons**:
- Slow to build; 3–6 months before meaningful traffic
- Lower engagement than Instagram or TikTok
- Czech Pinterest penetration is lower than Western Europe
- Requires separate content creation workflow

**Czech-Specific Notes**:
- Czech Pinterest users heavily browse home decor and recipe content — coffee crosses both
- Cross-posting atlas articles to Pinterest boards could drive SEO-adjacent traffic
- Czech holiday/seasonal themes (Advent, Velikonoce) do very well on Pinterest

**YourWave Fit**: ★★★☆☆ — Tertiary channel. Activate Month 4–6 as a low-effort SEO complement.

---

### 2.4 Email Marketing

**CZ Audience**: Owned channel — list built by YourWave. Czech email open rates average 24–28% (slightly above EU average).

**CPA Estimate**: €2–8 per conversion once list established (lowest CPA of all channels)

**Pros for Coffee DTC**:
- Highest ROI channel for DTC brands (industry average: €42 return per €1 spent)
- Direct relationship — no algorithm dependency
- Perfect for subscription retention, new lot announcements, re-engagement
- Segmentation enables tailored messaging (subscribers vs. prospects vs. churned)
- Czech consumers respond well to personalized content; low spam culture vs. Western markets

**Cons**:
- Requires building list first — takes time
- Requires quality content to avoid unsubscribes
- Czech users are protective of email addresses; lead magnet needed for sign-ups
- GDPR compliance is strict in CZ — proper opt-in flows required

**Czech-Specific Notes**:
- Czech email tools: Ecomail.cz (Czech-native, GDPR-compliant, €15–25/month for 1K subscribers)
- Alternative: Mailchimp (free up to 500 contacts) or Klaviyo (best for Shopify DTC)
- Lead magnet ideas: Free brewing guide PDF, coffee quiz result, atlas article PDF

**YourWave Fit**: ★★★★★ — Equal priority to Instagram. Start building list from Day 1.

### 2.5 SEO / Content Marketing

**CZ Audience**: Google has 97% search engine market share in CZ. Coffee-related Czech searches: ~180K/month (estimate).

**CPA Estimate**: €3–12 long-term (once content ranks); effectively €0 marginal cost per visitor

**Pros for Coffee DTC**:
- Atlas content is already a natural SEO asset
- Czech coffee terms are LOW competition — most coffee content is in English
- Ranks permanently vs. paid channels that stop when budget stops
- Compounds over time — every article published increases organic footprint
- Czech specialty coffee education content: almost no competition at article level

**Top Czech Coffee Search Terms (estimated)**:
- "jak připravit filtrovanou kávu" — ~1,200/month
- "specialty coffee Praha" — ~880/month
- "nejlepší káva na doma" — ~720/month
- "Aeropress recept" — ~590/month
- "Ethiopia Yirgacheffe" — ~340/month (CZ)
- "káva předplatné" — ~210/month (subscription intent!)

**Cons**:
- 6–18 months to see meaningful results
- Requires consistent writing / atlas content publishing
- Technical SEO setup needed (meta tags, schema, site speed)
- Competitive internationally even if CZ-local is open

**Czech-Specific Notes**:
- Czech Google TLD (.cz) gets preference for CZ searches — hosting and domain matter
- Hreflang tags needed if bilingual (CS + EN)
- Czech specialty coffee bloggers are few — outreach for backlinks is easier than in English market

**YourWave Fit**: ★★★★☆ — Start immediately with Atlas content. Long-term competitive moat.

### 2.6 Google Ads

**CPA Estimate**: €15–45 per subscription conversion (CZ CPC for coffee terms: €0.40–1.80)

**Pros**:
- Intent-based — captures people actively searching for coffee subscriptions
- Immediate traffic
- Measurable ROAS from day one
- Shopping ads effective for product discovery

**Cons**:
- Expensive relative to <€500/month budget
- "Kávové předplatné" search volume is small in CZ — limited scale
- Requires conversion tracking setup and testing period to optimize
- Not appropriate for early-stage pre-product-market-fit brands

**Czech-Specific Notes**:
- CZ Google Ads market is cheaper than DE/UK/FR — advantage if you do activate
- Local search ads for "specialty coffee Praha" could work if driving café-adjacent traffic
- Brand search protection (own name) worth bidding on once brand grows

**YourWave Fit**: ★★☆☆☆ — Do not activate until Month 6+, once email and organic are established.

### 2.7 Heureka.cz and Mall.cz (Czech Marketplaces)

**CZ Audience**: Heureka: 4.2M monthly visitors; Mall.cz: 3.1M monthly visitors

**CPA Estimate**: 5–15% commission on sales (no upfront CPA but margin compression)

**Heureka.cz**:
- Czech price comparison engine — drives 35% of all CZ e-commerce decisions
- Coffee products listed but specialty segment underdeveloped
- Users are price-conscious; bad for premium positioning
- Good for volume but destroys brand perception at entry price points
- Heureka Košík (cart) integration allows direct purchase

**Mall.cz**:
- Czech-Slovak e-commerce marketplace (owned by GUS Group)
- Coffee category exists but commodity-focused
- Margins compressed: 15–25% commission
- Useful for brand discovery but limits DTC relationship building
- No subscription model available on platform

**Pros for Coffee (generally)**:
- High-trust platform — Czech consumers buy from Heureka-verified sellers readily
- Built-in audience; no need to build traffic
- Reviews system builds social proof

**Cons for YourWave specifically**:
- Subscription model not supported
- Premium positioning hard to maintain on price-comparison platform
- Customer relationship owned by marketplace, not YourWave
- No atlas/education integration possible

**YourWave Fit**: ★★☆☆☆ — Consider for one-time bag sales only, never for subscription funnel. Low priority.

## 5. Czech-Specific Considerations

### Local Platform Notes
- **Heureka.cz**: Czech eBay equivalent for discovery — consider listing coffee products at Month 4+ for awareness only, not as primary sales channel
- **Zboží.cz** (Seznam): Second price comparison engine; Czech-owned; worth listing on once Heureka is live
- **Fler.cz**: Czech Etsy-equivalent — good for artisan/handcraft positioning; specialty coffee could fit
- **Czech Facebook groups**: "Kávové forum CZ" and similar groups are very active; genuine community participation (not spam) builds brand awareness
- **Ecomail.cz**: Czech email marketing tool with better local support and CZ GDPR templates than Mailchimp

### Czech Consumer Behavior
- Czech consumers are research-intensive before purchase — SEO and atlas content are crucial
- Price sensitivity is real but specialty segment customers have higher willingness-to-pay than market average
- Word-of-mouth and personal recommendations are the #1 conversion driver in CZ
- Czech consumers trust verified reviews (Heureka Ověřené hodnocení) — collect reviews early
- Subscription model is less common in CZ than Western Europe — education is needed

### Regulatory Notes
- Czech GDPR enforcement is active — ensure proper consent flows for email
- Czech Trade Inspection (ČOI) monitors online sellers — ensure terms and delivery SLA are clear
- VAT registration threshold: CZK 2M (~€80K) — not immediate issue but plan for it

## 6. Summary Recommendation

**For YourWave at <€500/month, the winning formula is:**

1. **Instagram + Email as co-primary channels** — Instagram drives awareness and community; Email converts and retains
2. **Atlas/SEO as long-term foundation** — every article published is a compounding asset
3. **TikTok as secondary growth engine** — first-mover advantage in CZ coffee education niche
4. **No paid ads until Month 5+** — organic proof of concept first
5. **Avoid marketplaces** — they commoditize the brand and kill the DTC relationship

**North star**: When a customer lands on YourWave, they should feel like they discovered a brand — not bought a product. All channel choices should reinforce that feeling.
