## 2. PROBLEM STATEMENT

### The core problem

Czech and Central European consumers face a fundamental disconnection between the coffee they buy and the food system that produced it. The market is structurally bifurcated:

**Tier 1 — Commodity supermarket coffee:** Brands like Jacobs, Nescafé, and Tchibo dominate retail shelf space. These products are cheap, convenient, and entirely opaque: no origin story, no producer relationship, no freshness guarantee, and margins that leave farmers with less than 2% of the final retail price.

**Tier 2 — Specialty café culture:** Prague's third-wave café scene is world-class. Independent cafés like EMA Espresso Bar, Kavárna Co Hledá Jméno, and Double Shot serve outstanding coffee — but they are inaccessible to consumers outside the city centre, require a physical visit, and have made no systematic effort to educate or serve home brewers through direct channels.

**The gap:** There is no DTC-native specialty coffee brand built for the Czech consumer who wants exceptional coffee at home, understands enough to care about origin and freshness, but lacks the time or location to visit specialty cafés regularly. This consumer is growing rapidly — but they are currently served by either supermarket shelves or the occasional order from foreign subscription services (European competitors like Pact Coffee or The Coffee Collective) that offer no Czech-language education, no local market knowledge, and no community.

### Scale of the problem

- Czech coffee market: approximately €340 million annually (CZK 8.5B+), projected to reach €437 million by 2028
- Specialty segment: estimated at 3–5% of total market today, but the fastest-growing segment — the Czech Republic is identified by the Centre for the Promotion of Imports (CBI) as one of Eastern Europe's leading specialty coffee markets
- DTC coffee penetration in Czech Republic: nascent — no domestic DTC-native specialty brand with meaningful market share exists
- Consumer education gap: most Czech consumers cannot identify the difference between washed and natural processed coffee, cannot name a single coffee-producing region in Ethiopia, and have never seen the farm that grew their beans
- Sustainability gap: the average Czech coffee consumer has no visibility into the price paid to the farmer, the working conditions, or the environmental practices of their coffee supplier

### Why this matters to EIT Food's mission

EIT Food's mandate is to create a more sustainable, trustworthy, and healthy food system. The current Czech coffee supply chain fails on all three dimensions: it is environmentally opaque (no traceability), economically extractive (farmer prices suppressed by commodity trading), and nutritionally uninspiring (stale, commodity-grade beans). YourWave directly addresses each dimension: traceable origin, direct-trade pricing to farmers, and a content platform that builds consumer food literacy around one of the world's most complex agricultural products.

## 3. SOLUTION AND INNOVATION

### What YourWave is

YourWave is a specialty coffee direct-to-consumer brand with an embedded educational platform, built natively for the Czech market and designed to scale across Central Europe.

**Core product offering:**

| Product Line | Description | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Wave Origins | Single-origin coffees, sourced farm-direct | 200g whole beans |
| Morning Wave | House blend, approachable and consistent | 200g whole beans |
| Explorer Wave | Subscription — new origin every 6–8 weeks | 200g + brew card |
| Limited Wave | Micro-lots and competition-grade lots | 100g, limited release |
| Drip Bags | Pre-portioned pour-over bags, no equipment needed | 10-pack |

**Supply chain model:**
- Green coffee sourced via Algrano (direct farm connection, transparent pricing) and Jaká Káva (Czech specialty importer)
- Contract roasting with Prague/Brno specialty roasters: Chroast, Industra, The Miners
- Freshness guarantee: roasted-to-order or within 7 days of shipment
- Sold exclusively DTC online via yourwave.uk — no wholesale, no retail intermediaries in v1

### The Atlas: what makes this genuinely different

The Atlas (atlas.yourwave.uk) is a structured, SEO-optimised content library covering every dimension of specialty coffee: producing countries, varietals, processing methods, brew guides, tasting vocabulary, and producer profiles. It currently contains 62 English-language articles and is being extended into Czech.

**Why the Atlas is a strategic moat, not just a content blog:**

1. **Organic customer acquisition:** When a Czech consumer searches "what is washed processing" or "best Ethiopian coffee," Atlas articles appear in search results — driving discovery with zero paid acquisition cost. This is a tested content-commerce model (pioneered by brands like Trade Coffee in the US) applied to the underserved Czech market.

2. **Customer education as retention:** A subscriber who understands why their Kenyan Karogoto tastes of blackcurrant is far less likely to churn than one who just received "a bag of coffee." Education creates attachment to the category and to the brand.

3. **Scalability across markets:** The Atlas content library is language-agnostic in its architecture. Czech localisation is underway; Polish, Slovak, and German versions are feasible extensions with minimal incremental cost.

4. **Differentiation vs. all competitors:** No competitor in the Czech or Slovak market combines DTC coffee sales with a structured educational content engine at this scale. This is a genuine content-commerce integration that competitors cannot replicate quickly.

### Innovation dimensions

**Digital innovation:** Content-commerce integration at the SKU level — every product page links directly to the relevant Atlas articles (origin profile, producer story, brew guide). This creates a seamless educational journey from discovery to purchase to brewing to advocacy.

**Supply chain innovation:** Direct sourcing via Algrano gives YourWave access to real-time farm pricing, lot information, and producer profiles — data that feeds directly into product storytelling and into Atlas content. This is transparency as a product feature, not a marketing claim.

**Business model innovation:** The subscription flywheel — content drives organic discovery, education increases conversion, subscription provides recurring revenue, subscription data informs sourcing and content priorities, content drives more discovery. Each component reinforces the others.

**Sustainability innovation:** Direct-trade sourcing with published price premiums (target: 20–40% above commodity price to farmer), minimal packaging (kraft paper, no plastic laminates), and a carbon footprint commitment built into the Atlas's producer profiles.

## 8. SOCIAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT

This section directly addresses EIT Food's evaluation criteria on sustainability and food system transformation. It should be emphasised prominently in the application.

### Direct trade and farmer economics

The commodity coffee system is structurally extractive. The New York C Market price — which determines what most of the world pays for coffee — frequently falls below the cost of production for smallholder farmers, particularly in periods of oversupply. Farmers growing specialty-grade coffee (scoring 80+) have no systemic mechanism to capture the premium their quality deserves, because commodity traders buy based on weight, not cup score.

YourWave's sourcing model directly addresses this:

- **Algrano platform:** connects YourWave directly with coffee farms and cooperatives, with publicly visible prices paid to the farmer. The Algrano model guarantees farmers see the actual sale price, not a commodity price set by intermediaries
- **Price premium commitment:** YourWave targets purchasing at 20–40% above the prevailing C Market commodity price for equivalent origin and grade
- **Published transparency:** every product page on yourwave.uk will display the price paid to the farm per kg of green coffee — a radical transparency commitment that no mainstream coffee brand in the Czech market makes

### Supply chain traceability

Every coffee YourWave sells is traceable to farm level:
- Farm name, region, and producer profile
- Processing method, varietal, harvest year
- Cup score (where available from Algrano or importer)
- Price paid to farmer

This directly addresses the EU Farm to Fork Strategy's traceability objectives and aligns with EIT Food's mandate to create a more transparent food system.

### Packaging and waste

- Primary packaging: kraft paper bags with aluminium-free one-way valve — recyclable in Czech municipal waste streams
- No plastic laminates in v1 packaging
- Drip bag format: individual sachets paper-sealed — compostable wrapper target for v2
- No single-use capsules — YourWave explicitly does not compete in the pod/capsule market

### Consumer food literacy

The Atlas is, at its core, a food literacy project. An EU consumer who understands what they are drinking — where it was grown, who grew it, how it was processed, why it tastes the way it does — is a more empowered, more discerning, and ultimately more sustainable consumer. They are more likely to pay fair prices, less likely to default to commodity purchasing, and more likely to demand transparency from other food producers.

YourWave's goal is not just to sell coffee. It is to build a generation of Central European consumers who understand food provenance and demand it from every food system they interact with.

| European Green Deal | Minimal packaging, no capsules, sustainable sourcing |
