# Instagram Strategy for YourWave — CZ Specialty Coffee Brand

## Executive Summary

Instagram remains the primary visual discovery platform for specialty coffee in Central Europe. For YourWave, a Czech DTC specialty coffee brand, Instagram offers the highest ROI among social channels for building brand identity, educating consumers, and driving direct sales. This document outlines a comprehensive strategy based on current platform algorithms, Czech market behaviour, and benchmarks from leading global specialty coffee brands.

**Key recommendations at a glance:**

- Post Reels 3x per week (primary growth driver in 2025–2026)
- Stories daily (retention and community)
- 3–5 hashtags per post (algorithm shift away from 30-tag approach)
- Lead with education + origin storytelling
- Localise with Czech language content; use English for international reach posts
- Target Czech specialty coffee hashtags + pan-European coffee community

---

## CZ Market Context

The Czech coffee market is growing at **2.36% annually through 2028**, with approximately 14,000 tonnes of green coffee imported per year. Third-wave specialty culture is expanding rapidly in Prague, Brno, and Ostrava, with a growing segment of consumers willing to pay premium prices for traceable, high-quality single-origin coffee.

**Consumer profile for Czech specialty coffee buyers:**

- Age 25–40, urban, educated, higher disposable income
- Values transparency, sustainability, origin traceability
- Increasingly interested in home brewing (V60, AeroPress, espresso)
- Influenced by European specialty coffee culture (Scandinavian brands, UK roasters)
- Social media discovery is primary channel for new brand awareness

**Platform preference in CZ:**

- Instagram: dominant for food/beverage discovery (70%+ of relevant demographic)
- TikTok: growing, especially under 30
- Facebook: declining relevance for specialty segment
- Pinterest: niche but effective for recipe/brewing content

## Content Pillars

YourWave's Instagram content should be structured around five core pillars. Each pillar serves a distinct audience need and maps to a different stage of the customer journey.

### Pillar 1: Origin Stories (Educate + Inspire)

_Target: awareness and brand differentiation_

Content ideas:

- Producer portraits from Ethiopia, Colombia, Panama
- Farm-to-cup journey videos (15–30 second Reels)
- Regional terroir explainers ("Why Yirgacheffe tastes like blueberries")
- Processing method visuals (washed vs. natural drying beds)
- Atlas article teasers ("We wrote a deep dive — link in bio")

Posting frequency: 2–3x per week, mix of Reels and carousels

### Pillar 2: Brewing Craft (Educate + Convert)

_Target: consideration and purchase intent_

Content ideas:

- Pour-over technique Reels (under 60 seconds)
- "Brew ratio explained" carousel posts
- Grind size comparison visuals
- Water temperature guides
- "What your coffee method says about you" (engagement bait, shareable)

Posting frequency: 1–2x per week

### Pillar 3: Product Focus (Convert)

_Target: direct purchase_

Content ideas:

- New release announcements with cupping notes
- Packaging detail shots (lifestyle + flat lay)
- Limited edition / seasonal lot drops
- "This week's featured bag" Stories with swipe-up to shop
- Customer unboxing reposts

Posting frequency: 1x per week (avoid over-selling)

### Pillar 4: Community + Culture (Retain + Grow)

_Target: loyalty and word-of-mouth_

Content ideas:

- Czech café partner features
- Customer coffee setups (#KávaDoma community posts)
- Prague/Brno specialty café recommendations
- Coffee event coverage (Barista championships, tastings)
- Behind-the-scenes at YourWave (roasting, cupping, team)

Posting frequency: 2–3x per week in Stories, 1x per week in feed

### Pillar 5: Values + Sustainability (Trust)

_Target: brand loyalty among values-driven consumers_

Content ideas:

- Farmer relationship posts (fair pricing, direct trade)
- Environmental commitment (packaging materials, carbon footprint)
- Certifications and sourcing transparency
- B-roll from origin trips

Note: Eco-content generates approximately +47% higher engagement in the European food/beverage segment. This is a significant competitive advantage if executed authentically.

## Posting Strategy

### Recommended Cadence

| Format         | Frequency           | Purpose                       |
| -------------- | ------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Reels          | 3x per week         | Reach and discovery           |
| Carousel posts | 2x per week         | Education and saves           |
| Static image   | 1x per week         | Aesthetics and brand identity |
| Stories        | Daily (5–10 frames) | Retention and community       |
| Live           | 1x per month        | Events, tastings, Q&A         |

### Optimal Posting Times (CET)

- Weekdays: 7:00–9:00 (morning coffee ritual), 12:00–13:00 (lunch), 19:00–21:00 (evening browse)
- Weekends: 9:00–11:00 (leisure time, higher engagement)

### Reels Best Practices

- Hook in first 1–2 seconds (no slow intros)
- 15–45 seconds ideal length for coffee content
- Original audio OR trending Czech/Slovak audio
- Subtitles for silent viewing (70% of mobile users)
- End with a clear CTA: "Přečti celý příběh — odkaz v biu" or "Objednej na yourwave.cz"

## Hashtag Strategy

As of 2025, Instagram's algorithm deprioritises posts using 20–30 hashtags. The optimal range is **3–5 highly relevant hashtags** per post.

### Czech-language hashtags

- `#výběrovákáva` — core specialty coffee term in CZ
- `#kávadoma` — home brewing community
- `#kávičkáři` — Czech coffee enthusiast community
- `#praskakáva` — Prague coffee scene

## Competitor Benchmarks

### Global specialty brands active on Instagram

**La Cabra Coffee (Denmark)**

- Strategy: minimalist aesthetics, origin-focused storytelling, high production quality Reels
- Engagement: consistent 2–4% engagement rate
- Posting: 5–6x per week across formats
- Lesson: Quality over quantity; every post is visually considered

**Square Mile Coffee (UK)**

- Strategy: education-first content, cupping notes as carousels, community transparency
- Tone: knowledgeable but approachable; not pretentious
- Lesson: Trust-building through transparency works better than lifestyle marketing for specialty

**Tim Wendelboe (Norway)**

- Strategy: deep origin content, producer relationships, minimal promotional posts
- Reels: farm visits, processing, roasting process
- Lesson: Producer partnerships generate the most authentic and shareable content

**Onyx Coffee Lab (US)**

- Strategy: aggressive Reels, trending audio, educational hooks
- Growth: fastest-growing specialty brand on Instagram in 2024
- Lesson: Willingness to engage with platform trends while maintaining specialty credibility

### Czech market — white space

Currently, no Czech specialty coffee brand has cracked the formula for consistent, high-quality Instagram storytelling. This is YourWave's opportunity to own the space.

## Recommendations

1. **Launch with an origin series** — start with Ethiopia (highest emotional resonance), film a virtual farm journey using existing imagery and the Atlas content as a script
2. **Build the #KávaDoma community** — create a Czech home-brewing hashtag and actively repost customer content
3. **Invest in Reels production** — one well-produced Reel per week outperforms five static posts
4. **Localise everything** — Czech language captions first, English optional. The Czech specialty audience responds significantly better to native language content
5. **Link Atlas to Instagram** — every educational Instagram post should drive to the corresponding Atlas article; use "odkaz v biu" as the CTA
6. **Monthly lives** — cupping sessions, origin Q&As, or "ask the roaster" will build community faster than any paid campaign
7. **Track saves and shares** — not just likes. Saves indicate educational value; shares indicate virality potential

## KPIs to Track (Monthly)

- Reach (target: +15% MoM for first 6 months)
- Follower growth rate (target: +8–12% MoM)
- Engagement rate (target: ≥3% — industry benchmark for food/bev)
- Story views retention (target: ≥70% completion rate)
- Link-in-bio clicks (primary conversion metric)
- Saves per post (content quality indicator)
- Top-performing Reels topics (inform future content planning)
