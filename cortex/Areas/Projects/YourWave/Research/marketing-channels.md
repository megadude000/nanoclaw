# Marketing Channel Comparison — YourWave CZ Specialty Coffee DTC

## Overview

This document evaluates the five primary digital marketing channels for YourWave as a Czech DTC specialty coffee brand: Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, Email Marketing, and SEO. Each channel is assessed across six dimensions: reach potential, cost efficiency, conversion effectiveness, content requirements, audience quality, and strategic fit for specialty coffee.

**Conclusion up front:** No single channel dominates. YourWave should operate a layered strategy with Instagram as the primary brand channel, SEO as the primary acquisition engine, and Email as the primary retention and revenue channel. TikTok and Pinterest are secondary bets.

---

## Channel Profiles

### 1. Instagram

**Reach Potential:** High — 1.3B+ active users globally; strong Czech urban demographic (25–40)

**Strengths:**

- Highest brand-building potential for visual/lifestyle products
- Strong specialty coffee community (#specialtycoffee, 12M+ posts)
- Reels algorithm favours new accounts — low barrier to discovery
- Shopping integration enables in-app purchases
- Stories create daily touchpoints with existing audience

**Weaknesses:**

- Declining organic reach for static posts (feed posts: ~2–5% reach)
- High creative investment required for Reels production
- Link friction — only one link in bio (mitigated by Linktree or native Instagram Shop)
- Algorithm volatility — strategy must adapt to platform changes

**Cost to execute:** Medium — primarily time investment for content creation; paid ads optional but effective for DTC conversion campaigns

**Conversion role:** Mid-funnel (awareness → consideration); direct conversion via Instagram Shop

**Czech-specific:** Urban Czech specialty coffee audience is active here. Prague/Brno café culture has Instagram presence. Czech language content significantly outperforms English-only for domestic audience.

**Best for:** Brand identity, community building, organic discovery, influencer partnerships

---

### 2. TikTok

**Reach Potential:** Very High — fastest-growing platform globally; Czech Republic: 2.8M users, skews 18–34

**Strengths:**

- Highest organic reach of any social platform in 2024–2025
- Algorithm is non-follower-based — viral potential from day one
- Authentic, lo-fi content performs as well as (or better than) polished production
- Massive coffee content community (#coffeetok: 8.5B+ views)
- Educational content (brew tutorials, origin explainers) performs exceptionally well

**Weaknesses:**

- Younger audience (18–28) — partial overlap with specialty coffee buyer (typically 25–40)
- Content format requires different approach — faster, more casual than Instagram
- Shorter content lifecycle — posts lose relevance quickly
- Shopping integration less mature in Czech Republic than Western markets
- Czech TikTok specialty coffee niche is underdeveloped (opportunity but also risk)

**Cost to execute:** Low to Medium — lo-fi content works; investment is time and consistency

**Conversion role:** Top-of-funnel (awareness and discovery); weak at direct conversion currently

**Czech-specific:** CZ TikTok coffee space is nearly empty — first-mover advantage available. Czech Gen Z coffee consumers (the next wave of specialty buyers) are primarily on TikTok.

**Best for:** Brand awareness, reaching younger cohort, experimental content, viral moments

**Strategic fit:** Secondary channel. Dedicate 2–3 Reels-equivalent posts per week repurposed from Instagram content. Monitor performance; scale if traction emerges.

---

### 3. Pinterest

**Reach Potential:** Medium — 518M monthly active users globally; Czech presence is moderate

**Strengths:**

- Highest purchase intent of any social platform (72% of users report buying after seeing a pin)
- Long content lifecycle — pins drive traffic for months or years
- Strong for recipe/brewing/lifestyle content
- Evergreen SEO value — Pinterest pins rank in Google Image Search
- Less competitive than Instagram for specialty coffee in Czech Republic

**Weaknesses:**

- Smaller Czech-specific audience (Pinterest under-indexes in CZ vs. Western Europe)
- Platform skews female (60%+), which may not match YourWave's primary demographic
- Lower social proof component — less community/conversation than Instagram or TikTok
- Requires consistent pinning volume (10–25 pins/day for algorithm favour)

**Cost to execute:** Low — content can be repurposed from Instagram; automation tools available

**Conversion role:** Mid-to-bottom funnel — high purchase intent, especially for gift sets and subscriptions

**Best for:** Seasonal campaigns, gift-giving content, brewing recipe discovery, evergreen traffic

**Strategic fit:** Tertiary channel. Auto-publish feed posts and blog content to Pinterest. Minimal additional effort if systems are set up correctly.

---

### 4. Email Marketing

**Reach Potential:** Lower than social (list size dependent), but extraordinarily high quality

**Strengths:**

- Highest ROI of any digital marketing channel: average €36–42 return per €1 spent
- Direct channel — no algorithm dependency
- Owned audience — not at risk from platform changes
- Subscription model is ideal for email (weekly coffee drops, new lot announcements)
- Advanced segmentation enables personalised communication at scale
- Highest conversion rates for repeat purchases

**Weaknesses:**

- Requires list building — initial investment in lead generation
- Deliverability management required (avoid spam filters)
- Creative fatigue if content is not varied
- List growth is slower than social follower growth

**Cost to execute:** Low to Medium — email platform (Klaviyo, Mailchimp) + copywriting time

**Conversion role:** Bottom-funnel primary — highest direct revenue driver of any channel

**Key flows to implement:**

- Welcome series (3-email onboarding with origin story + first purchase discount)
- Abandoned cart recovery (2–3 touch sequence)
- Post-purchase flow (brew guide, origin story, review request)
- New lot announcement (subscription list + all buyers of that origin)
- Monthly digest (new arrivals, Atlas content, community highlights)

**Czech-specific:** Czech email marketing has lower average open rates (18–22%) than UK/US, but specialty coffee subscribers tend to be highly engaged when content is relevant and educational.

**Best for:** Customer retention, repeat purchases, subscription conversion, revenue maximisation

**Strategic fit:** Primary revenue channel. Invest in list building from day one; every Instagram and TikTok CTA should point to email signup.

---

### 5. SEO (Search Engine Optimisation)

**Reach Potential:** High and compounding — Czech Republic: 7.6M Google search users

**Strengths:**

- Highest-intent traffic of any channel (users are actively searching for solutions)
- Compounding returns — content published today generates traffic for years
- Low ongoing cost once content is established
- YourWave Atlas content is a natural SEO asset — educational content ranks well
- Czech coffee search volume is growing: "výběrová káva" searches up 34% YoY
- Local SEO potential for Prague/Brno café and online delivery searches

**Weaknesses:**

- Slow to start — meaningful rankings typically take 6–18 months
- Requires consistent content production and technical SEO maintenance
- Google algorithm changes can affect rankings
- Competitive for generic coffee terms; opportunity in long-tail specialty queries

**Target keyword clusters:**

- Informational: "výběrová káva co to je", "jak uvařit pour over", "arabika vs robusta"
- Commercial: "koupit výběrovou kávu online", "single origin káva česká republika"
- Navigational: "YourWave káva", "YourWave recenze"
- Local: "výběrová káva Praha", "specialty coffee Praha doručení"

**Atlas content as SEO moat:** The 30+ Atlas articles (now available in Czech) are pre-built SEO assets. Each article targets an informational keyword cluster. This is a significant competitive advantage — no other Czech specialty coffee brand has this depth of educational content.

**Czech-specific:** Czech language SEO is less competitive than English. Ranking for Czech specialty coffee terms requires less domain authority than equivalent English queries. Early investment has outsized returns.

**Best for:** Sustainable organic traffic, high-intent customer acquisition, long-term brand authority

**Strategic fit:** Primary acquisition channel alongside Instagram. Treat the Atlas as an SEO-first content library.

## Strategic Summary

**Phase 1 (Months 1–3): Foundation**

- Launch Instagram with consistent posting schedule
- Set up email capture and welcome flow
- Publish CZ Atlas articles with SEO-optimised metadata

**Phase 2 (Months 4–6): Growth**

- Scale Reels production; test TikTok with repurposed content
- Build email list to 500+ subscribers
- Monitor Atlas article rankings; optimise top performers

**Phase 3 (Months 7–12): Optimisation**

- Email sequences generating consistent repeat revenue
- SEO traffic beginning to compound
- TikTok decision: scale or maintain minimal presence based on data
- Instagram community established; consider micro-influencer partnerships
