## Notes Before Sending

**Tone calibration for Czech B2B:**
Czech business culture tends toward directness and professional formality on first contact, but is not cold. Avoid the Anglo-American habit of excessive positivity and hollow compliments ("I've been a huge admirer of your brand for years"). State your purpose clearly, ask specific questions, be brief. A short, specific email gets a faster response than a long, flattering one.

**Founder-direct tone:**
These templates are written from a founder, not a procurement manager. This is intentional — in the Czech specialty scene, most suppliers and roasters are also founder-run. Peer-to-peer tone opens doors faster than corporate language.

**Subject lines:**
Keep them factual. Czech inboxes respond to clarity, not clickbait.

## Template 2 — Algrano — Green Coffee Supplier

**Supplier context:** Swiss direct-trade marketplace platform. Warehouses in Bremen, Germany. Fully transparent pricing (FOB + fee shown at checkout). Q-grader evaluated at listing, pre-shipment, and arrival. 300+ verified producers. ~4 months from order to delivery. Best supplier for brand storytelling — producer photos, farm details, payment transparency all provided by platform. Platform has a "Requests" feature to specify needs.

Note: Algrano is a platform, not a traditional importer. Contact is via their website/platform registration. This template is designed as a platform onboarding inquiry + partnership interest note.

---

**Subject:** New Roaster Account — YourWave (Czech Republic, Specialty DTC Brand)**

---

Dear Algrano team,

My name is [Name]. I am the founder of YourWave, a specialty coffee DTC brand launching in the Czech Republic. Our model is subscription and direct online sales, with a strong emphasis on origin storytelling and traceability — which is precisely why Algrano caught my attention.

I am in the process of creating an account on the platform and have a few questions before my first order:

1. What is the typical minimum quantity per lot for a first-time buyer? I see the platform mentions as low as 3 bags — is this consistently available, or lot-dependent?
2. What is the estimated timeline from placing an order to coffee arriving at my location in Czech Republic (via Bremen)? I understand approximately 4 months — is this accurate for EU deliveries?
3. Do you offer sample orders before committing to a full lot? If so, what is the process?
4. Is the "Requests" feature the best way to specify particular origins or processing methods I am looking for?
5. Are there any account setup requirements I should know about before placing my first order?

We are looking to source 2–3 single-origin lots to start — primarily Ethiopia and Colombia, with interest in experimental processes (naturals, anaerobic, carbonic maceration). Volumes will be small initially (3–5 bags) but we are building a brand that will grow its sourcing with its subscriber base.

The transparency Algrano provides — producer names, payment details, supply chain data — is central to how we plan to tell our brand story to customers. I look forward to building a relationship with the platform and the producers on it.

Thank you.

[Name]
Founder, YourWave

## Template 3 — Chroast — Contract Roaster (Hradec Králové)

**Roaster context:** Chmelík family + Filip Kopecký. Founded ~2018. First specialty roaster in Hradec Králové. Micro-roastery, small-batch, fruit-forward profiles. NOT Prague as initially assumed — located in Hradec Králové. Contract roasting is NOT advertised — this is a cold inquiry to explore feasibility. Scale may be limiting. Treat as a relationship-building contact, not a primary production partner.

---

**Subject:** Spolupráce — zakázkové pražení pro novou DTC značku YourWave

---

jmenuji se [Jméno] a zakládám YourWave — českou specialty coffee DTC značku s online prodejem a předplatným. Hledáme pražírnu pro zakázkové (toll) pražení — dodáváme vlastní zelené kafe, vy jej pražíte na náš profil.

Váš přístup k pražení — zejména ovocné, sweet profily — sedí dobře s tím, co chceme nabízet.

1. Nabízíte zakázkové (toll) pražení pro cizí značky?

Začínali bychom s malými objemy — řádově 5–20 kg/měsíc — ale hledáme partnera pro dlouhodobou spolupráci, ne jednorázové zakázky.

Pokud zakázkové pražení standardně nenabízíte, rád/a bych věděl/a, jestli je to vůbec varianta k diskuzi, nebo zda doporučujete jiný typ spolupráce.

Equipment: Diedrich IR-12 (12 kg batch). Roasts Tuesdays and Wednesdays. Has wholesale program for offices and businesses. Contract roasting not advertised — this is a cold inquiry. Strong quality and expertise reputation. London specialty coffee background = high standards. 12 kg batch capacity is the key constraint for volume scaling.

---

**Subject:** Contract Roasting Partnership Inquiry — YourWave Specialty Coffee DTC

---

Dear Petra and Adam,

My name is [Name]. I am founding YourWave, a specialty coffee DTC brand based in [city], Czech Republic, launching later this year. We are a subscription and direct e-commerce business — customers receive single-origin specialty coffees with detailed origin stories, roasted fresh to order.

## Template 5 — The Miners — Contract Roaster (Prague)

**Roaster context:** Founded 2019 by Egor Kolpakov & Oldřich Valta. DOCK Prague 8, roastery + HQ. 31 coffee shops across Europe, aggressive expansion (20 new locations in 2026). 40+ B2B partners including WeWork, TedX. Roasts 3x/week. Has B2B portal (b2b.theminers.eu). Capsule production capability. Won Best Design at BCN Coffee Awards 2023. Featured in Forbes. NOT primarily a contract roaster — their core business is brand expansion and franchise. Free B2B delivery on orders >300 EUR CZ, >500 EUR EU. Strong specialty reputation.

Key strategic note: The Miners are growing their own brand aggressively. A contract roasting request may not interest them, but a B2B wholesale partnership (buying their roasted coffee under co-branding arrangement) might. The template below is written to open a conversation about multiple models.

---
