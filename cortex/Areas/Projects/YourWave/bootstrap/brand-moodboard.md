# Brand Identity Moodboard — Global Specialty Coffee DTC Brands

## Purpose

This document analyses the brand identities of 12 leading global specialty coffee DTC brands to extract patterns, visual languages, tonal strategies, and positioning approaches relevant to YourWave. The goal is not to copy any single brand, but to map the design and brand space, identify white space, and build a considered identity direction for YourWave's Czech market positioning.

---

## Brand Profiles

### 1. La Cabra Coffee Roasters (Denmark)

**Positioning:** Quiet luxury. The most aesthetically refined specialty coffee brand in Europe.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Off-white, warm cream, forest green, pale terracotta
- Typography: Clean sans-serif with generous spacing; occasionally paired with elegant serif for editorial moments
- Photography: Daylight-only, film-like quality, minimal props, negative space dominant
- Packaging: Matte paper, embossed logo, restrained colour blocking by origin

**Tone of voice:** Sparse, poetic, factual. Never breathless or hyperbolic. "Washed. Ethiopia. Jasmine. Bergamot. Soft."

**What they do brilliantly:** Make silence feel luxurious. Their brand communicates quality without ever shouting it.

**Lesson for YourWave:** Visual restraint signals confidence. Less copy, more intention.

---

### 2. Square Mile Coffee Roasters (UK)

**Positioning:** The specialist's specialist. Built by World Barista Champions for serious coffee people.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Clean white, black, occasional accent colours for specific lots
- Typography: Bold geometric sans-serif; strong grid layouts
- Photography: Process-focused — cupping, measuring, roasting machinery
- Packaging: Minimal information architecture; lot code, origin, process clearly prioritised

**Tone of voice:** Direct, knowledgeable, uncompromising. Written as if by someone who has read every coffee study ever published.

**What they do brilliantly:** Authority. Nobody questions their credibility because every detail of their brand confirms it.

**Lesson for YourWave:** The Atlas positions YourWave as an educator. Lean into this. Knowledge is a brand asset.

---

### 3. Tim Wendelboe (Norway)

**Positioning:** The farmer-first roaster. Producer relationships as the core product.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Earthy neutrals — tan, stone, ochre, forest green
- Typography: Humanist sans-serif; warm and approachable
- Photography: Origin-first — farm workers, processing stations, drying beds, landscape
- Packaging: Kraft paper, hand-drawn origin maps, detailed lot information

**Tone of voice:** Personal, honest, specific. Named producers. Named farms. Named altitudes. No vague "artisanal" language.

**What they do brilliantly:** Making the invisible visible — the supply chain becomes the story.

**Lesson for YourWave:** Origin storytelling is the brand differentiator in specialty coffee. Atlas content + Instagram = this strategy executed digitally.

---

### 4. Onyx Coffee Lab (USA)

**Positioning:** Premium craft meets contemporary brand culture. Specialty coffee for the design-literate consumer.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Deep black, warm gold, rich jewel tones for seasonal lots
- Typography: Premium display serifs for headlines; clean sans-serif for body
- Photography: High production value, dramatic lighting, studio-quality composition
- Packaging: Substantial matte black bags with foil accents; collectible feel

**Tone of voice:** Passionate, detailed, accessible. Enthusiastic without being alienating.

**What they do brilliantly:** Made specialty coffee feel like a luxury product without losing its craft credibility.

**Lesson for YourWave:** Packaging as a first unboxing moment. The bag someone receives at home is a brand statement.

---

### 5. Stumptown Coffee Roasters (USA)

**Positioning:** The original third-wave brand that went mainstream without losing its soul (mostly).

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Warm red, tan, heritage-influenced colour combinations
- Typography: Mix of vintage hand-lettering and functional sans-serif
- Photography: People-first — baristas, farmers, customers; coffee as part of life
- Packaging: Illustrated, story-driven, each bag tells a narrative

**Tone of voice:** Warm, irreverent, human. Not talking at you, talking with you.

**What they do brilliantly:** Made specialty coffee feel approachable rather than intimidating.

**Lesson for YourWave:** Czech consumer psychology leans sceptical of pretension. Warmth and approachability will outperform cold minimalism in the Czech market.

---

### 6. Blue Bottle Coffee (USA/Japan)

**Positioning:** The tech world's specialty coffee brand. Precision meets ritual.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: White, pale sky blue, minimal
- Typography: Clean geometric sans-serif; scientific precision in layout
- Photography: Ultra-minimal studio shots; single cup, single beam of light
- Packaging: White, simple, the blue bottle logo immediately recognisable

**Tone of voice:** Meditative, precise, ritualistic. Coffee as mindful practice.

**What they do brilliantly:** Created a ritual identity — buying Blue Bottle feels like joining a philosophy.

**Lesson for YourWave:** The subscription model (ritual) is the product, as much as the coffee itself.

---

### 7. Workshop Coffee (UK)

**Positioning:** The architect's coffee brand. Design-forward, urban, intelligent.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Monochrome with occasional bold accent
- Typography: Architectural grid systems; condensed sans-serif
- Photography: Geometric composition, café interiors as architecture
- Packaging: Restrained, structured, information as design

**Tone of voice:** Precise, professional, respectful of the reader's intelligence.

**What they do brilliantly:** Treating coffee retail like a design discipline.

**Lesson for YourWave:** For a Prague urban audience, design sophistication signals quality as much as the coffee itself.

---

### 8. Mokka (Czech Republic)

**Positioning:** Pioneer Czech specialty roaster; established brand with café presence.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Earthy brown, cream, warm tones
- Typography: Traditional, legible
- Photography: Classic café culture — warm, inviting
- Packaging: Professional but conservative

**Tone of voice:** Knowledgeable but not cutting-edge in its communication.

**What they do brilliantly:** Brand recognition in Czech market through longevity and café presence.

**Lesson for YourWave:** There is space in the Czech market for a more visually contemporary, digitally native specialty brand. Mokka owns the "established" position; YourWave can own "progressive."

### 9. Nomad Coffee (Spain)

**Positioning:** European specialty with Mediterranean openness. Coffee as exploration.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Warm whites, terracotta, olive, deep navy
- Typography: Humanist typefaces with travel-inspired illustration elements
- Photography: Origin travel, market scenes, Mediterranean light
- Packaging: Illustration-led, joyful, collectible

**Tone of voice:** Curious, warm, adventurous. Every coffee is an invitation to travel.

**What they do brilliantly:** Made specialty coffee feel like an adventure rather than a transaction.

**Lesson for YourWave:** "Atlas" as a brand concept maps perfectly to this exploration narrative. Lean into the geography of coffee as a brand story.

### 10. September Coffee (Netherlands)

**Positioning:** Understated Dutch design applied to specialty coffee.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Muted pastels, pale sage, soft warm whites
- Typography: Minimal, highly legible
- Photography: Lifestyle-first — coffee in beautiful domestic settings
- Packaging: Soft matte, gentle colour coding by origin

**Tone of voice:** Quiet, considered, unhurried.

**What they do brilliantly:** The "coffee at home" lifestyle aspiration — their imagery makes you want to be at home with their coffee.

**Lesson for YourWave:** DTC subscription model should be communicated through "the ritual of your morning" rather than product features.

### 11. Belleville Brûlerie (France)

**Positioning:** Parisian third-wave with European cultural depth.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: Deep charcoal, cream, terracotta, occasional vibrant accents
- Typography: Mix of classic French typography with contemporary layout
- Photography: High-contrast, cinematic
- Packaging: Premium, tactile, gift-worthy

**Tone of voice:** Cultural, worldly, refined without arrogance.

**What they do brilliantly:** Made specialty coffee feel like French cultural patrimony.

**Lesson for YourWave:** Czech cultural context (Kafkaesque depth, Central European sophistication) is an underused asset in coffee branding. YourWave could own this narrative.

### 12. Hario (Japan, globally distributed)

**Positioning:** The equipment brand that became a lifestyle brand.

**Visual identity:**

- Palette: White, red, minimalist Japanese design language
- Typography: Precision, technical but beautiful
- Photography: Process-focused, meditative
- Packaging: Functional, considered, internationally recognisable

**What they do brilliantly:** Made coffee equipment aspirational; became the V60 name itself.

**Lesson for YourWave:** Brand-equipment-education integration. YourWave Atlas teaches technique → YourWave recommends equipment → YourWave sells coffee for the equipment. Full ecosystem play.

## Pattern Analysis

### Colour Palette Trends Across Top Brands

**Dominant patterns:**

- Warm neutrals: cream, off-white, bone (10/12 brands use as foundation)
- Earth tones: terracotta, ochre, brown (8/12 brands)
- Deep anchors: forest green, charcoal, deep navy (7/12 brands)
- Accent use: gold, vibrant jewel tones used sparingly for premium signalling

**What's rare and ownable:**

- Vibrant, saturated palettes (specialty coffee has settled into warm neutrality)
- Cold, contemporary palettes (blue, grey) — almost absent in specialty coffee
- Anything that feels genuinely Central/Eastern European

### Typography Patterns

- **Humanist sans-serif** dominates (approachable, readable, neutral)
- **Geometric sans-serif** for more technical/precision-positioned brands
- **Serif** used increasingly for editorial moments, packaging headlines
- Trend: pairing a workhorse sans with a character-rich display face

### Photography Language

- Natural light preferred by 10/12 brands
- Origin photography (farms, processing) present in 9/12
- People (not just product) in 8/12
- Ultra-minimal studio/product shots in 4/12 brands

### Packaging Principles

- Matte surfaces dominant (matte = craft; gloss = commodity)
- Tactile materials signal premium quality
- Information hierarchy: origin → variety → process (before brand name in some cases)
- Collectibility principle: makes consumers want to display the bag

## White Space — YourWave Opportunity

Based on this analysis, the following brand positioning territory is open in the Czech/Central European specialty coffee market:

1. **Digital-native educational brand** — no Czech brand has positioned education (via Atlas) as its core differentiator
2. **Central European cultural depth** — coffee brands in Prague have copied Scandinavian/UK aesthetics; Czech cultural identity is unexplored
3. **Atlas-as-brand** — the exploration/geography narrative maps perfectly to a DTC specialty brand
4. **Warm contemporary design** — between La Cabra's cold minimalism and Stumptown's heritage warmth; something that reads as "Czech sophistication"

---

## Brand Direction Recommendation for YourWave

**Visual direction:**

- Foundation palette: warm cream / parchment / off-white + deep forest / slate + terracotta accent
- Typography: humanist sans-serif for body (approachable) + editorial serif for headlines (authority)
- Photography: origin-first, natural light, people visible (not anonymous hands), domestic ritual
- Packaging: matte, substantial, origin-mapped, collectible as a series

**Tone of voice:**

- Curious and knowledgeable, never pretentious
- Czech language primary; English secondary
- Specific always (named farms, named producers, named altitudes) — vague is cheap
- Warm but not casual; educated but not exclusionary

**Brand position:**
_YourWave is the atlas of specialty coffee — a brand that explores, explains, and brings exceptional coffee from around the world to Czech homes._
