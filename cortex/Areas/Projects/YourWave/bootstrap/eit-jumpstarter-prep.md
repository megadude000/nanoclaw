# EIT Jumpstarter 2026 — Application Preparation for YourWave

## Program Overview

**EIT Jumpstarter** is a pre-acceleration program run by the European Institute of Innovation and Technology (EIT Food), designed to support early-stage food and agri-food startups across Europe. It is one of the most accessible and relevant EU funding opportunities for YourWave at its current stage.

**Key facts:**

- Program type: Pre-acceleration (non-equity)
- Duration: 7 months (typically June–December)
- Prize structure: €2,500 / €5,000 / €10,000 (based on track and milestone achievement)
- Application deadline: **May 8, 2026** (confirmed for 2026 cohort)
- Czech Republic: **Eligible** — EIT Food Central hub covers CZ/SK/PL/HU
- Equity taken: None — grant-based funding
- Prior EIT funding limit: ≤ €60,000 total received to date (YourWave is under this threshold)
- Legal entity requirement: No S.R.O. required at application stage — sole traders and pre-incorporation teams accepted
- Language: English (application and program)

## Program Structure

EIT Jumpstarter runs in phases:

**Phase 1 — Online Application (now → May 8, 2026)**

- Application form + pitch deck + video pitch
- Describe problem, solution, market, team, traction

**Phase 2 — Selection (May–June 2026)**

- Shortlisted teams invited to online interviews
- ~80–120 teams selected from hundreds of applications

**Phase 3 — Bootcamp (June–July 2026)**

- Intensive hybrid bootcamp (online + one in-person event)
- Workshops: business model, customer discovery, investor pitching
- Mentorship from EIT Food network

**Phase 4 — Acceleration (July–November 2026)**

- Bi-weekly mentor sessions
- Milestone-based progress tracking
- Access to EIT Food partner network (major food companies, investors, retailers)

**Phase 5 — Demo Day + Prizes (November–December 2026)**

- Final pitch event
- Prize allocation: €2,500 (participation), €5,000 (strong progress), €10,000 (top performer)

## Application Components

### 1. Problem Statement

**What to communicate:**

Czech and Central European consumers are disconnected from specialty coffee's origin, story, and quality. The market is bifurcated: commodity supermarket coffee at one end, inaccessible specialty café culture at the other. There is no accessible, educational DTC brand bringing transparent, traceable specialty coffee directly to homes — with the knowledge layer to help consumers appreciate what they're drinking.

**Key data points to include:**

- Czech coffee market: €340M+ annually, growing 2.36% per year
- Third-wave specialty segment: <5% of total market but fastest growing
- DTC penetration in Czech food/beverage: still nascent compared to UK/US/NL
- Consumer education gap: most Czech consumers cannot distinguish Arabica varieties or processing methods

---

### 2. Solution / Innovation

**What to communicate:**

YourWave is a specialty coffee DTC brand with an embedded educational layer — the Atlas. Unlike standard coffee subscription services, YourWave treats every bag of coffee as a gateway to knowledge: the origin story, the producer, the processing method, and the brew technique are all accessible via the Atlas platform, linked directly from product pages.

**Innovation angle:**

- Content-commerce integration: Atlas articles reduce customer acquisition cost via organic SEO
- Community-building: educational content creates advocates, not just customers
- Scalable model: the Atlas content library scales across languages and markets
- Sustainability narrative: traceable supply chain + producer storytelling

---

### 3. Market & Traction

**Market size:**

- Czech coffee market: ~€340M annually
- Addressable specialty segment: ~€17–34M (5–10% of total)
- DTC online food/bev in CZ: growing 18% YoY post-COVID
- EU specialty coffee market: €4.5B+, growing 8% annually

**Traction to demonstrate (build before May 8):**

- Atlas platform live with 30+ CZ-language educational articles
- Website with waitlist or early-adopter sign-ups
- Social media presence (Instagram followers, engagement data)
- Any letters of intent from potential customers or café partners
- Market validation: survey data from target customers (even 50–100 responses is meaningful)

---

### 4. Business Model

**Revenue streams:**

1. Direct coffee sales (primary): single origin bags, seasonal lots, subscriptions
2. Subscription model (recurring revenue): monthly / bi-monthly coffee subscription
3. Future: Atlas premium content, brewing masterclasses, wholesale to specialist retailers

**Unit economics to prepare:**

- Average order value
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) — even estimated
- Lifetime value (LTV) — modeled
- Gross margin on coffee bags (specialty DTC typically 50–65% gross margin)
- Subscription retention rate target

---

### 5. Team

**What EIT Jumpstarter assesses:**

- Complementary skills (technical, commercial, domain expertise)
- Commitment level (full-time vs. part-time founders)
- Domain credibility (specialty coffee knowledge, food industry background)
- Coachability (openness to mentorship and feedback)

**How to present YourWave's team:**

- Lead founder: background in specialty coffee + digital/content
- Technical capacity: Atlas platform development
- Market knowledge: Czech food/bev landscape
- Advisors: if none yet, note that you are building an advisory board through the EIT Jumpstarter network

---

### 6. Pitch Deck Structure (Recommended)

1. **Cover:** YourWave — Specialty Coffee, Explored (with tagline)
2. **Problem:** The disconnection between Czech consumers and exceptional coffee
3. **Solution:** Specialty DTC + Atlas educational platform
4. **Product:** Coffee offering + Atlas demo screenshots
5. **Market:** Czech specialty coffee TAM/SAM/SOM
6. **Traction:** Atlas live, articles count, social following, any pre-orders
7. **Business model:** Revenue streams, unit economics, subscription model
8. **Go-to-market:** Instagram + SEO + Email strategy (3-channel flywheel)
9. **Roadmap:** 6-month milestones (what Jumpstarter funding will accelerate)
10. **Team:** Founders, roles, relevant background
11. **Ask:** Joining EIT Jumpstarter + what you'll achieve with the program

---

### 7. Video Pitch

EIT Jumpstarter typically requires a 2–3 minute video pitch. Best practices:

- Founder on camera (not slides) — authenticity matters
- Show the product: bags, Atlas platform, brewing ritual
- Speak to the problem personally: "When I first tried Ethiopian Yirgacheffe, I realised I knew nothing about where my coffee came from..."
- Clear ask: "We're joining EIT Jumpstarter to accelerate our route to market and connect with Europe's food innovation ecosystem"
- End with website or social handle
