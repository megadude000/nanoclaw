## 1. Executive Summary

YourWave is a direct-to-consumer specialty coffee subscription brand built for home coffee enthusiasts who want to discover exceptional coffees from around the world. Based in Prague, Czech Republic, the brand operates on a contract-roasting model in Phase 1, eliminating the need for roasting equipment capital while maintaining full control over sourcing, branding, and customer experience.

The core differentiator is the **Coffee Atlas** — a 62-article multilingual educational platform (EN/CS/UK) that drives organic traffic, builds brand authority, and converts readers into subscribers. This content-commerce flywheel is unusual in the Czech market and creates a defensible moat against commodity coffee brands.

**Phase 1 snapshot:**
- Team: 2 (founder + partner for brand/content/social)
- Model: Contract roasting (no capital equipment required)
- Target market: Czech Republic, English-speaking expats, Czech specialty enthusiasts
- Funding sought: EIT Jumpstarter €10K (non-equity grant, deadline May 8, 2026)
- Path to first sale: 6 months from now
- 18-month revenue target: CZK 460,000 (~€18,400) at Month 18 (500 subscribers)

### 2.2 Specialty Coffee Segment

The specialty coffee segment in Czech Republic is early-stage but growing rapidly. Key facts:

- **Penetration:** Less than 2–5% of total coffee market by volume; higher by value given premium pricing
- **Growth:** Outpacing the overall market — specialty coffee consumer awareness is expanding as café culture matures in Prague and other cities
- **Addressable specialty market:** Conservatively €9–23M per year (2–5% of €459M total), with realistic growth to €40M+ by 2028 at current trajectory
- **EU context:** Europe's branded coffee shop market grew 4.7% in 2024/2025, with 51,042 outlets in 2025 (up from 40,675 in 2021). Czech Republic benefits from this regional momentum.

### 2.4 Market Opportunity Gap

The Czech specialty coffee DTC subscription market has a clear gap:

- **No English-first specialty subscription** serving Prague's large expat community
- **No discovery-led model** with educational content integrated into the purchase journey
- **No brand** combining traceable sourcing stories with a curated subscriber experience
- Closest competitors (Kofio, Doubleshot) serve Czech-speaking audiences; none lead with educational content-commerce integration

## 4. Business Model

### 4.1 Phase 1 Structure (Months 1–12)

```
[Green Coffee Supplier] → [Contract Roaster (CZ)] → [YourWave Packing] → [Zásilkovna/DPD] → [Customer]
```

- YourWave buys green coffee from suppliers (Jakafe, Algrano, or Nordic Approach)
- Contract roaster (Industra Coffee, The Miners, or Chroast) roasts to YourWave's spec
- YourWave bags, labels, and packs orders in rented storage space
- Ships via Zásilkovna (pickup points, ~89 CZK) or DPD (~68 CZK) within CZ

### 4.2 Revenue Streams

| Stream | Phase | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| One-time coffee sales | Phase 1 | Single-bag purchases via e-shop |
| Monthly subscriptions | Phase 1 | Core recurring revenue model |
| Quarterly subscriptions | Phase 1 | Prepaid, discounted ~10% |
| Starter Wave Box | Phase 1 | Introduction kit (3 origins × 100g) |
| Gift subscriptions | Phase 1 | Gifting vertical, high AOV |
| Limited Wave drops | Phase 1/2 | Microlot limited editions, higher margin |

### 4.3 Product Lines

|------|-------------|--------|-------|
| Wave Origins | Rotating single origins by country | 250g, 500g bags | Phase 1 |

### 4.4 Content-Commerce Flywheel

```
Coffee Atlas article (SEO) → Organic traffic → Email capture → Welcome sequence → First order → Subscription
        ↑                                                                                              |
        └──────────────────── New origin added → new article → new social content ──────────────────-┘
```

The Atlas drives zero-cost customer acquisition. Each new coffee origin added to the product range generates a corresponding Atlas article, which drives SEO traffic, builds email list, and converts to subscribers.

## 5. Unit Economics

### 5.1 COGS Breakdown — Per 250g Unit (Explorer Wave, Standard)

All costs in CZK (exchange rate: 1 EUR = 25 CZK approximation).

| Cost Component | Low | Mid | High | Notes |
|---------------|-----|-----|------|-------|
| **Green coffee** | 35 CZK | 50 CZK | 75 CZK | €4–8/kg standard specialty; €8–12/kg premium. Assumes ~50% weight loss in roasting. 250g finished = ~385g green needed. |
| **Contract roasting fee** | 75 CZK | 100 CZK | 125 CZK | €3–5/kg roasted estimate (market rate for CZ contract roasters). Per 250g = €0.75–1.25 |
| **Bag (specialty valve bag)** | 12 CZK | 15 CZK | 20 CZK | ~€0.50–0.80 per bag at low volume. Prices drop significantly at 1,000+ units. |
| **Box/mailer** | 8 CZK | 12 CZK | 18 CZK | Corrugated mailer box for shipping |
| **Label (custom printed)** | 3 CZK | 5 CZK | 8 CZK | Digital print, per unit at low volume |
| **Insert card** | 2 CZK | 4 CZK | 6 CZK | Origin story card, printed |
| **Tape, tissue, extras** | 2 CZK | 3 CZK | 5 CZK | Branding consumables |
| **Shipping (Zásilkovna CZ pickup)** | 89 CZK | 89 CZK | 109 CZK | 89 CZK base rate pickup points; 109+ home delivery. Fuel surcharge ~12.5% additional. Effective: ~100 CZK |
| **Payment processing (2.5%)** | 12 CZK | 16 CZK | 20 CZK | On 480–800 CZK order value |
| **Platform/subscription software** | 5 CZK | 8 CZK | 12 CZK | Shopify Basic €29/mo amortized over orders |
| **TOTAL COGS per 250g unit** | **243 CZK** | **302 CZK** | **398 CZK** | |

**Key assumption on green coffee cost:**
- Standard specialty (SCA 83–85): ~€4–6/kg green → ~€8–12/kg after roasting loss
- Premium specialty (SCA 86+): ~€8–12/kg green → ~€16–24/kg after roasting loss
- Per 250g finished unit: green cost = 35–75 CZK depending on origin tier

### 5.6 Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

LTV calculations at different churn rates (monthly subscription, 490 CZK/month):

| Monthly Churn | Avg. Lifetime (months) | LTV (revenue) | LTV (gross profit at 40%) | LTV:CAC ratio (at 400 CZK CAC) |
|--------------|----------------------|--------------|--------------------------|-------------------------------|
| 15% | 6.7 months | 3,283 CZK | 1,313 CZK | 3.3:1 |
| 10% | 10 months | 4,900 CZK | 1,960 CZK | 4.9:1 |
| 7% | 14.3 months | 7,007 CZK | 2,803 CZK | 7.0:1 |
| 5% | 20 months | 9,800 CZK | 3,920 CZK | 9.8:1 |

**Target:** Achieve <7% monthly churn (industry benchmark for specialty food subscription is 8–12%). YourWave's discovery model and educational content should drive below-average churn by increasing perceived value.

**LTV:CAC benchmark:** Healthy SaaS/subscription benchmark is 3:1 minimum. Even at 15% churn (pessimistic), YourWave achieves 3.3:1. At 7% churn (realistic target), LTV:CAC is 7:1 — excellent.

## 11. Risk Analysis

### 11.1 Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| High churn rate (>15%/month) | Medium | High | Invest in post-purchase experience (insert cards, origin emails, brewing tips). Monitor NPS monthly. |
| Contract roaster quality inconsistency | Medium | High | Establish quality standard in writing. Cup every batch before shipping. Use 2 roasters as backup. |
| Green coffee price volatility | High | Medium | Diversify across 2–3 suppliers. Price review every 6 months. Premium tier absorbs volatility better. |
| Slow Atlas SEO traction | Medium | Medium | Atlas is long-term asset; supplement with Instagram in early months. Don't rely solely on SEO pre-launch. |
| Low EIT Jumpstarter conversion | High | Low | Grant is a bonus, not a dependency. Launch plan works without it. |
| Legal/SZPI delays | Low | Medium | Start registration process 3 months before launch. S.R.O. takes 2–4 weeks; allow buffer. |
| Packaging quality issues | Medium | Medium | Order sample run before full batch. Test heat seal integrity on valve bags. |
| Czech market too small | Low | High | 500 subscribers = €18K MRR at Month 18 — very achievable in CZ. EU expansion is Phase 2 natural path. |
| VAT threshold crossed too early | Low | Medium | At 500 subs + ~€18K MRR, annual run rate is ~€220K — above 2M CZK threshold. Register for VAT proactively at ~€80K/year. Build 12% VAT into pricing from day one. |

### 11.2 Coffee Price Pressure Note

Green coffee commodity prices were elevated in 2024–2026 due to weather impacts in Brazil and Vietnam. For specialty-grade Arabica (the segment YourWave operates in), the supply/demand dynamics are somewhat insulated from commodity C-market volatility, but not immune. YourWave should:

- Build a 6–8 week inventory buffer for green coffee
- Lock in prices with Algrano or Jakafe for 3-month rolling commitments where possible
- Pass through extraordinary cost increases via transparent "crop news" communications to subscribers (this is actually good content and builds trust)
