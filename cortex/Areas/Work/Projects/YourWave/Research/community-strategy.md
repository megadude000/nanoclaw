**Status:** Score 0/100 — Starting from zero

**Purpose:** Research and roadmap for building YourWave's community from scratch

## 1. Why Community Matters for a Specialty Coffee Brand

Community is the flywheel that makes content-to-customer work. For YourWave:
- Coffee Atlas educates → community creates belonging → belonging drives brand loyalty → loyalty drives purchases
- Community members become brand ambassadors with zero paid acquisition cost
- In Czech Republic, specialty coffee is still a niche: being the connective tissue of that niche gives a durable competitive moat

The core insight from successful specialty coffee brands: **community precedes commerce**. The brands that win (Blue Bottle early days, Onyx Coffee Lab, The Barn Berlin) built an audience of believers before they built a sales funnel.

## 2. How Specialty Coffee Brands Build Communities

### 2.1 Global DTC Coffee Brands — Community Channels

**Discord**
- Used by: Onyx Coffee Lab, deeper niche roasters, the "coffee nerds" segment
- Best for: Real-time discussion, brewing help, cupping notes sharing
- Audience: High-engagement, tech-forward, 25-40 demographic
- Barrier: Requires active moderation and seeding; dead Discords are worse than no Discord
- YourWave fit: Strong (Coffee Atlas content is perfect Discord fuel)

**Telegram**
- Used by: European specialty coffee communities, eastern European coffee groups
- Best for: Broadcast channel + community group hybrid, newsletter alternative
- Audience: Privacy-conscious, engaged subscribers
- Barrier: Low — easy to start, lower expectation of polish
- YourWave fit: Excellent for Czech/Slovak market specifically

**Email Lists**
- Used by: Counter Culture, Intelligentsia, James Hoffmann, every serious coffee educator
- Best for: Long-form content, offer announcements, relationship building
- ROI: Highest of any channel — email generates $36 for every $1 spent (DMA, 2023)
- YourWave fit: Primary long-term asset; Coffee Atlas is a natural email capture engine

**Events**
- Used by: The Barn (Berlin), Tim Wendelboe (Oslo), local roasters everywhere
- Types: Cuppings, brewing workshops, farm-to-cup talks, coffee festival presence
- Best for: Converting online followers to loyal offline community members
- YourWave fit: Prague has a rich event scene; events can happen before own product exists

**Instagram/TikTok Communities**
- Comment sections, DM conversations, UGC reposts = community signals
- Not a community channel itself but a discovery and funneling layer into real community

### 2.2 Community Mechanics That Work

| Mechanic | What It Does | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Weekly cupping notes | Creates ritual, drives repeat engagement | Counter Culture weekly posts |
| Member spotlights | Validates community members | Barista communities |
| Brewing challenges | Generates UGC, drives engagement spikes | #V60Challenge |
| Ask the roaster sessions | Builds trust and authority | Blue Bottle AMAs |
| Education drops | Gives members something to share | James Hoffmann YouTube |
| Private access / early products | Rewards loyal community members | Onyx early drops |

## 3. Prague Specialty Coffee Community Landscape

### 3.1 Existing Communities and Groups

**Physical community hubs:**
- Doubleshot (Praha 1) — oldest specialty roaster in CZ, strong community
- EMA Espresso Bar — known for barista education
- Rebelbean — popular with younger coffee crowd
- Ground Praha — specialty café with community feel
- Cafe Lounge (Vinohrady) — consistent specialty crowd

**Events:**
- Prague Coffee Festival (annual) — main gathering, 5,000-10,000 visitors, held in spring
- Barista Championship CZ (annual) — competition circuit, connects serious baristas
- World Barista Championship (Czech has sent representatives) — national pride angle
- Pop-up cuppings hosted by Doubleshot, Rebelbean, others

**Online groups (Czech):**
- Facebook: "Kavičkáři" — Czech coffee enthusiasts group, ~10k members, active
- Reddit: r/kava (Czech) — smaller but engaged
- Instagram: Czech specialty coffee scene active under tags #kava #specialtycoffee #czechcoffee #prazskakava
- No dominant Discord or Telegram yet for CZ specialty coffee = white space

**Key figures/influencers:**
- Jan Nekvapil (SCA trainer, Prague)
- Czech Barista Championship medalists (names change yearly)
- Specialty café owners with Instagram followings

### 3.2 Gap Analysis

| What Exists | What's Missing |
|-------------|----------------|
| Individual cafés' communities | Brand-agnostic specialty community |
| Facebook groups (aging demographic) | Discord/Telegram for younger crowd |
| English-language global communities | Czech-language specialty community |
| Cupping events (sporadic) | Regular, accessible education events |
| Pro-focused barista content | Consumer-facing education (this is Coffee Atlas) |

**YourWave opportunity:** Be the first brand to build a Czech-language, consumer-friendly specialty coffee community. Coffee Atlas is the education layer; community is the belonging layer on top.

## 4. First 6 Months Community Plan

### Month 1: Foundation (No product, no brand identity needed)

**Week 1-2: Telegram channel launch**
- Create @YourWaveCZ Telegram channel
- First 20 posts: repurposed Coffee Atlas articles as short-form insights
- Announce to any existing personal network
- Add Telegram link to Coffee Atlas site

**Week 3-4: Email capture activation**
- Ensure email capture is live on Coffee Atlas (EmailCapture component)
- Create a welcome sequence: 5 emails over 2 weeks introducing YourWave's philosophy
- Goal: 50 subscribers by end of month 1

**Content seeds:**
- "The one thing that changed how I brew V60" (short insight)
- "Why your espresso is sour (and it's not the beans)" (Coffee Atlas crosspost)
- "Prague specialty coffee map" (original, highly shareable)

### Month 2: First Community Touchpoint

**Online:**
- Host first Telegram AMA: "Ask anything about coffee"
- Start a weekly "Brewing Tip Tuesday" format
- Begin Instagram presence (even without product — brand story + education content)

**Offline:**
- Attend Prague Coffee Festival as visitor — network, meet other enthusiasts
- Connect with 3-5 local café owners (potential Guest Wave partners later)
- Goal: First 5 genuine community relationships formed

**Metrics to track:** Telegram subscribers, email list size, Instagram followers, DM conversations

### Month 3: Content-Community Flywheel

**Launch: Weekly Coffee Atlas Digest**
- Email newsletter summarizing 2-3 Atlas articles + 1 original insight
- Should be forwarded/shareable — quality over quantity

**Community activation:**
- First community challenge: "Share your best brew setup photo" — simple, low barrier
- Feature 3 community member brews on Instagram

**Partnership exploration:**
- Reach out to 1-2 Prague cafés about hosting a joint cupping event
- No product needed — YourWave provides education, café provides space

### Month 4: First Event

**Hosting: Coffee Knowledge Evening**
- Format: 45-min brewing workshop + Q&A, 15-20 people
- Location: Partner café (free or minimal cost)
- Content: Based on Coffee Atlas science articles (extraction, grind, temperature)
- Ticket: Free or €5 (for commitment, donate to charity)
- Goal: 15 attendees, 10 newsletter signups, 2 Instagram posts from attendees

**Online parallel:**
- Discord launch (after Telegram establishes core community)
- Cross-post community content between Telegram/Discord/Instagram

### Month 5: Pre-Launch Community Building

**Waitlist activation:**
- "YourWave is launching. Join the inner circle."
- Community members get first access to products + founding member pricing
- Target: 200 email waitlist subscribers who are warm, educated, engaged

**Content escalation:**
- Monthly long-form essay (NotebookLM-quality research) on coffee topic
- Weekly short-form tip
- 3x/week social post

### Month 6: Community as Launch Engine

**Pre-launch:**
- Community members receive early access + handwritten (or personalized) notes
- Ask for honest feedback in exchange for free sample
- Generate 10+ genuine reviews before public launch

**Launch mechanics:**
- Community members get 48-hour exclusive access before public
- Invite community members to share — don't ask for promotion, ask for honest opinions
- Celebrate community in launch comms: "You made this possible"

## 5. How Coffee Atlas Creates Community Entry Point

### The Education → Belonging → Loyalty Funnel

```
Organic search / SEO
        ↓
Coffee Atlas article (reads, learns, bookmarks)
        ↓
Email capture ("Get weekly coffee insights")
        ↓
Welcome sequence (YourWave philosophy, story, values)
        ↓
Community invitation (Telegram / Discord)
        ↓
Event / AMA / challenge participation
        ↓
Product launch awareness → purchase
        ↓
Ongoing community engagement → repeat purchase → referral
```

### Atlas as Community Content Engine

Every Atlas article is:
1. A standalone educational resource (SEO value)
2. A conversation starter for community (share + discuss)
3. A credibility signal (you know this because you wrote it)
4. An email newsletter segment (curate weekly)

The 79 articles already live represent **79 community conversation seeds** waiting to be activated.

### Specific Atlas-to-Community Plays

| Atlas Article Type | Community Use |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Science articles (pressure, temperature) | Brewing tip newsletters, AMA prompts |
| Origin articles (Ethiopia, Colombia) | "Tasting this origin?" community threads |
| Process articles (washed, natural) | Explain what's in subscribers' subscriptions |
| Brewing method articles (V60, espresso) | Workshop curriculum |
| Variety articles (Gesha, SL28) | Cupping event themes |

## 6. Community Health Metrics

### Quantitative KPIs

| Metric | Month 1 Target | Month 3 Target | Month 6 Target |
|--------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Email subscribers | 50 | 150 | 400 |
| Telegram subscribers | 30 | 100 | 250 |
| Email open rate | 35%+ | 40%+ | 42%+ |
| Email click rate | 5%+ | 8%+ | 10%+ |
| Instagram followers | 100 | 400 | 1,000 |
| Event attendees (cumulative) | 0 | 20 | 60 |
| DM conversations/week | 2 | 10 | 20 |

### Qualitative Signals (equally important)

- Are community members talking to *each other* (not just to YourWave)?
- Are members sharing content without being asked?
- Are members asking questions that suggest genuine curiosity and trust?
- Are any members becoming de facto community hosts/helpers?
- Are cafés or other brands reaching out for collaboration?

### Red Flags

- Engagement rate below 2% on social
- Email open rate dropping below 25%
- Community channels with no member-initiated conversation for 7+ days
- No word-of-mouth signups (all growth is paid or SEO only)

## 8. Key Decisions Required

1. **Platform priority:** Telegram-first or Discord-first? (Recommend: Telegram first, Discord at month 3)
2. **Brand voice in community:** Educational authority vs. fellow enthusiast vs. founder sharing journey? (Recommend: founder sharing journey — most authentic for pre-product phase)
3. **Czech vs. English language:** Czech-first or bilingual? (Recommend: Czech-first with English Atlas articles; builds local differentiation)
4. **Event format:** Host own or partner with café? (Recommend: partner for first 2 events to reduce friction and access existing audience)
