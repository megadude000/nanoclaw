## 2. Equipment Research: Entry-Level Roasters (1-3 kg Batch)

### 2.1 Aillio Bullet R2 (1 kg batch)

**Overview:**
The Aillio Bullet R2 is an induction-heated drum roaster from Denmark. Industry standard for micro-roasters and serious home roasters. Uses IBTS (Infrared Bean Temperature Sensor) for direct bean temperature measurement — significantly more accurate than thermocouple-only systems.

**Specifications:**
- Batch size: 100g – 1,000g (1 kg max)
- Power: 1,600W induction heating (no gas required)
- Drum speed: Variable 2-12 RPM
- Airflow: Variable with 9 settings
- Weight: 28 kg
- Footprint: 470 x 470 mm
- Cooling tray: Integrated 1 kg capacity
- Software: Roast.World — full logging, profiling, replay, community recipes
- Price (2026): €2,500-3,000 (including VAT, EU pricing)

**Pros:**
- No gas connection needed — can run on standard 230V outlet
- IBTS gives best-in-class bean temperature data
- Roast.World software is excellent — recipe sharing, profile replay, cloud sync
- Strong community, huge recipe library online
- Apartment/small space friendly (well-vented area still needed)
- Resale value holds well (used market is active)

**Cons:**
- 1 kg max batch limits throughput (1 kg/25-30 min = ~2 kg/hour practical output)
- To produce 100 kg/month: ~50 roasting sessions, 25+ hours of roasting
- Not suitable as primary production roaster beyond ~50-80 kg/month
- Chaff collection needs regular cleaning
- Smoke: requires ventilation or Bullet-compatible afterburner (~€600 extra)

**Best for YourWave:** Perfect as a first own roaster — learning machine, sample roaster, small-batch specialty, R&D. Not the primary production machine at scale.

### 2.2 Hottop KN-8828B-2K+ (1 kg batch)

**Overview:**
Taiwanese roaster, long-established in the prosumer market. Has been the standard "first roaster" for many small operations. Electric drum roaster.

**Specifications:**
- Batch size: Up to 900g (effective ~600-700g for consistent results)
- Power: 1,600W electric
- Temperature probe: Two K-type thermocouples
- Software: Artisan compatible (open-source roasting software, free)
- Weight: 11 kg
- Price (2026): €700-900

**Pros:**
- Much cheaper than Bullet — entry point for experimentation
- Artisan software is free and very capable
- Lightweight and portable
- Good for learning fundamentals

**Cons:**
- Effective batch size smaller than rated (600-700g practical)
- Less precise temperature measurement than Bullet IBTS
- Build quality lower than Bullet or Giesen
- Limited to small-scale — throughput even lower than Bullet
- Community less active than Bullet's Roast.World ecosystem

**Best for YourWave:** Only relevant if budget is very constrained for R&D/learning phase. Not recommended as primary production tool.

### 2.3 Giesen W1A (1 kg batch)

**Overview:**
Dutch-made commercial roaster — Giesen is one of the most respected names in specialty roasting equipment. The W1A is their smallest model but built to full commercial standard. Gas-fired drum roaster.

**Specifications:**
- Batch size: 200g – 1,000g
- Heating: Gas (LPG or natural gas)
- Airflow: Variable with digital control
- Temperature probes: ET (exhaust) + BT (bean temp)
- Construction: Commercial-grade steel, Dutch manufacturing
- Software: Giesen profiling software (proprietary, also Artisan compatible)
- Footprint: Larger than Bullet — requires commercial space
- Price (2026): €8,000-12,000 (new)
- Weight: ~180 kg

**Pros:**
- True commercial build quality — will last 20+ years with maintenance
- Gas heating = faster heat-up, more even drum temperature, better for larger batches
- Exceptional roast consistency and repeatability
- Giesen service and parts network in Europe
- Scales with business (next model up is W6 at 6 kg)
- Professional credibility — many specialty roasters use Giesen

**Cons:**
- Price: 3-4x the Bullet for same 1 kg batch size
- Requires gas connection (LPG or piped natural gas)
- Requires commercial space (proper ventilation, fire safety, afterburner)
- Regulatory requirements significantly higher with gas equipment
- Overkill for Year 2 volumes unless raising capital

**Best for YourWave:** The right machine when scaling to 200+ kg/month and operating from a permanent commercial space. Year 3+ ambition.

## 3. Czech Regulatory Requirements for Operating a Coffee Roastery

### 3.1 Business Entity

**Required before any commercial food production:**
- Czech trade license (živnostenský list) for food production — category: "Výroba potravin" or specifically "pražení kávy"
- Registered with Czech Trade Inspection Authority (Česká obchodní inspekce)
- If operating as company: S.R.O. registration (already planned for YourWave)

### 3.2 SZPI Registration (State Agricultural and Food Inspection)

**SZPI (Státní zemědělská a potravinářská inspekce)** is the Czech food safety authority.

- All food producers must register with SZPI
- Registration is free — online form at szpi.gov.cz
- Requires: business address, description of activity, responsible person contact
- Timeline: ~2-4 weeks for registration confirmation
- Annual inspections possible — SZPI can visit unannounced

**Key regulation:** Czech Act No. 110/1997 Coll. on Foodstuffs and Tobacco Products

### 3.3 HACCP Plan

**Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP)** is mandatory for all Czech food producers.

For a coffee roastery, key HACCP elements:
- **Raw material hazards:** Mold risk in green coffee (Ochratoxin A — EU limit 5 μg/kg for roasted coffee)
- **Physical hazards:** Metal contamination, foreign objects
- **Chemical hazards:** Acrylamide formation during roasting (EU Regulation 2017/2158 — benchmark levels for coffee: 400 μg/kg roasted ground, 850 μg/kg instant)
- **Critical Control Points:** Green coffee inspection on receipt, roasting temperature control, packaging hygiene

**Practical HACCP for micro-roaster:**
- Document supplier quality certificates
- Log every roast (temperature profile = production record)
- Document cleaning procedures
- Maintain a basic pest control program
- Review and update annually

HACCP plan can be written internally or by a food safety consultant (~€500-1,500 for consultant-written plan)

### 3.4 Premises Requirements

**Minimum requirements for a food production premises:**
- Dedicated production space (cannot be a regular home kitchen)
- Adequate ventilation (roasting produces smoke — requires afterburner or industrial exhaust)
- Hygienic surfaces (washable walls/floors)
- Separate storage for raw materials (green coffee) and finished product
- Pest control evidence
- Access to running water and drainage
- Fire safety compliance (roasters are heat-producing equipment — fire extinguishers, smoke detectors required)

**For electric roasters (Bullet R2):**
- 230V/16A outlet minimum (dedicated circuit recommended)
- Ventilation for smoke — typically window + fan, or ducted exhaust
- Somewhat easier than gas — no gas connection permits needed

**For gas roasters (Giesen W1A):**
- Gas connection permit from local authority
- Czech Technical Standard ČSN requirements for gas appliances
- Mandatory inspection by licensed gas technician before operation
- Fire safety plan required
- Significantly more bureaucracy

### 3.5 Labeling Requirements

**Czech + EU labeling law for packaged roasted coffee:**
- Product name: "Pražená káva" or "Roasted Coffee" — both languages required on Czech market
- Net weight (in grams)
- Best before date (coffee: typically "Best before end of" + month/year)
- Country of origin — for blends: "Blend of coffees from different countries" or list countries
- Name and address of producer or importer
- Storage conditions: "Store in cool, dry place away from direct sunlight"
- Allergens: Coffee is not an allergen; no mandatory allergen box for pure coffee
- Nutrition declaration: Mandatory per EU Regulation 1169/2011

**Language requirement:** Czech law requires primary labeling in Czech. Additional languages permitted.

**Barcode:** Not legally required, but practically necessary for retail distribution (EAN barcode, €10-30/year from GS1 Czech)

### 3.6 Acrylamide Compliance

EU Regulation 2017/2158 applies to Czech Republic:
- Benchmark level for roasted coffee: 400 μg/kg
- Benchmark level for instant coffee: 850 μg/kg
- Not a maximum limit (legal production above benchmark is possible) but triggers corrective action if consistently exceeded
- Testing: Can send samples to accredited lab (~€100-200/test)
- Mitigation: Roasting at appropriate temperature/time (light roasts tend to have higher acrylamide; medium/dark lower)

## 4. Timeline and Investment Analysis

### 4.1 Phase 1: Contract Roasting (Now — Year 1)

**Model:** Pay contract roaster per kg roasted. YourWave supplies green coffee, receives roasted coffee back.

**Typical CZ contract roasting economics:**
- Contract roasting fee: €3-6/kg roasted (varies by roaster, relationship, volume)
- Green coffee cost: €10-25/kg depending on origin and quality
- Total COGS roasted coffee: €13-31/kg
- Retail selling price (YourWave): €35-60/200g = €175-300/kg
- Gross margin: ~60-75% (high, but no fixed cost absorption)

**Advantages:**
- Zero capex
- No regulatory burden for roasting (supplier's responsibility)
- Flexibility to switch origins, try new coffees
- Focus capital on brand and customer acquisition

**Disadvantages:**
- Lower control over roast profile
- MOQ constraints from roaster
- Margin shared with contract roaster
- Brand story less compelling ("we have our coffee roasted for us")

### 4.2 Phase 2: Own Roastery — Aillio Bullet R2

**Investment (Year 2, assuming 2027):**

| Item | One-time Cost |
|------|--------------|
| Aillio Bullet R2 | €2,800 |
| Afterburner/ventilation | €600 |
| Green coffee storage (bins, shelving) | €300 |
| Packaging equipment (heat sealer) | €200 |
| Scale (0.1g precision) | €150 |
| Artisan/Roast.World setup | €0-100 |
| HACCP plan (consultant) | €800 |
| SZPI registration | €0 |
| Barcode registration (GS1) | €150/year |
| Initial green coffee inventory (50 kg) | €1,000-1,500 |
| **Total Phase 2 startup** | **€6,000-7,500** |

**Ongoing monthly costs (Phase 2):**
- Electricity: ~€20-40/month (Bullet R2 is efficient)
- Premises: €0 if home-based (studio/garage), €200-500 if rented space
- Packaging materials: €0.50-1.50 per bag (depends on volume, supplier)
- Green coffee: Variable (COGS)

**Breakeven vs. Contract Roasting:**

Assume:
- Contract roasting saves €3/kg vs doing yourself
- Phase 2 fixed costs: €6,000-7,500 capex + €300/month operating

Breakeven (months) = €7,000 / (savings per month)
At 50 kg/month saving: €150/month savings → 47 months to break even
At 150 kg/month saving: €450/month savings → 16 months to break even
At 300 kg/month saving: €900/month savings → 8 months to break even

**Conclusion:** Own roastery makes financial sense when monthly volume exceeds ~200 kg consistently. At lower volumes, the time cost of roasting, quality learning curve, and capital tied up make contract roasting more efficient.

**The non-financial case for own roastery:**
- Full control over roast profiles
- Ability to offer truly unique coffees (single farm, micro-lots)
- Brand narrative: "We roast everything ourselves"
- Faster iteration when sourcing new coffees (no waiting for contract roaster slot)

### 4.3 Phase 3: Scale Up — Giesen W6 (6 kg batch)

**Trigger:** 300+ kg/month consistent volume, permanent commercial space, profitability proven

**Investment estimate:**
- Giesen W6: €25,000-35,000
- Commercial premises (rent or purchase): Major variable
- All regulatory requirements at full commercial level
- Timeline: Year 4-5 (2029-2030)

## 5. Green Coffee Sourcing Deep Dive

### 5.1 The Sourcing Ladder

```
Level 1: Commodity/spot purchase (lowest quality, lowest involvement)
Level 2: Importer relationship (curated lots, transparent sourcing, certifications)
Level 3: Direct trade co-op relationship (visit farm, multi-year commitment, premium)
Level 4: Direct farm relationship (exclusive lots, co-development)
```

YourWave Phase 1-2 target: **Level 2**, with selective Level 3 for flagship offerings.

### 5.2 Green Coffee Importers in Czech Republic and EU

**Czech Republic / Slovakia:**

**Jaká káva (Praha)**
- Model: Importer + retailer for specialty roasters and home roasters
- Origins: Ethiopia, Colombia, Guatemala, Kenya, Peru, Brazil
- MOQ: 5 kg (smallest in CZ market)
- Pricing: Transparent, published on website (~€8-18/kg depending on origin)
- Quality: SCA certified, sample-forward approach
- YourWave fit: Best first supplier — lowest MOQ, CZ-based, easy logistics
- Contact: jakakava.cz

**Doubleshot (Praha) — not a pure importer, but buys for resale to other roasters**
- Can sometimes supply green coffee to partner roasters
- Relationship-based — not a standard wholesaler

**EU Importers (ship to CZ):**

**Algrano (Switzerland — marketplace model)**
- Model: Direct trade marketplace connecting farms directly with roasters
- How it works: Browse lots from farms worldwide, buy direct, Algrano handles logistics
- MOQ: Typically 60-300 kg per lot
- Pricing: Farm-set prices + Algrano fee (~8-12% commission)
- Quality: Farmer-certified lots, full transparency (farm name, altitude, process, cupping score)
- Story value: Maximum — "direct from farm" is the best brand narrative possible
- YourWave fit: Excellent for flagship "Wave Origins" line; higher MOQ is the barrier
- algrano.com

**Trabocca (Netherlands)**
- Model: Traditional specialty importer, EU-based
- Origins: Strong in Ethiopia, Kenya, Colombia, Guatemala
- MOQ: 60 kg bags (1 jute sack)
- Pricing: Not public; requires account and quote
- Quality: Top-tier, SCA Q-grader verified lots
- Long-term: Ideal for building supplier relationships at scale
- trabocca.com

**Nordic Approach (Norway)**
- Model: Specialty importer, well-known in European specialty scene
- Origins: Africa-focused (Ethiopia, Kenya, Rwanda, Uganda), Latin America
- MOQ: 60-150 kg
- Quality: Very high, known for direct farmer relationships
- Story value: Strong "Nordic approach to sourcing" brand alignment possibility
- nordicapproach.no

**Sucafina (Switzerland)**
- Model: Large global trader with specialty division
- MOQ: Higher volumes (not ideal for Phase 1)
- Quality: Good, wide origin coverage

**DRWakefield (UK/EU)**
- Model: Traditional specialty importer with EU warehouse
- Origins: Very wide coverage
- MOQ: 60 kg
- Quality: High, SCA verified
- drwakefield.com

### 5.3 Building Direct Farm Relationships

**Why direct relationships matter for YourWave:**
- Strongest brand story ("we flew to Yirgacheffe and met Worku Gedefa who grows our coffee")
- Price stability — negotiate multi-season contracts
- Access to exclusive micro-lots before they appear on marketplace
- Quality feedback loop — your roasting feedback improves next harvest

**How to start (realistic for Phase 2-3):**

1. **Start with Algrano** — it's a structured direct-trade marketplace. Every purchase is technically "direct from farm." Build the story from Day 1.

2. **Request samples from 3-5 farms per origin you care about.** Cupping notes → select best. This is how professional sourcing works.

3. **Visit 1 origin per year (Year 2+).** Ethiopia and Colombia are most accessible for first trips. Jaká káva, Nordic Approach, and Trabocca often offer producer trip opportunities to roasters they work with.

4. **Multi-season commitment.** Tell a farm you want to buy 200 kg/year for 3 years if the quality is consistent. This unlocks exclusivity conversations.

5. **SCA Producer membership** (once at appropriate scale) — gives access to SCA's global producer network.

### 5.4 Key Origins for YourWave — Sourcing Strategy

**Ethiopia (flagship — Coffee Atlas already covers Yirgacheffe, Sidamo):**
- Story: birthplace of coffee, most complex aromatics in the world
- Best sourcing: Algrano has Ethiopian farms listed directly; Trabocca has long Ethiopia relationships
- Target: 1-2 Ethiopian origins in permanent lineup

**Colombia (accessibility + branding):**
- Hugely recognizable, reliable quality, good consumer familiarity
- Jaká káva stocks Colombian year-round
- Consider: A washed Colombian (accessibility) + a natural/honey processed (adventure)

**Kenya (for adventurous customers):**
- Bright, complex, wine-like — very distinctive
- Nordic Approach specialty
- Premium positioning — not for everyday lineup, for "Explorer Wave"

**Brazil (espresso blend component):**
- Nutty, chocolatey, low acidity — espresso blend foundation
- Multiple importers; Trabocca strong here
- Practical necessity if offering an espresso-friendly option

### 5.5 Certifications and Traceability

| Certification | What It Means | Value to YourWave |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Organic (EU) | No synthetic pesticides/fertilizers | Premium positioning, specific customer segment |
| Fairtrade | Minimum price guarantee to farmers | B2C marketing value, some customer expectation |
| Rainforest Alliance | Environmental and social standards | Less powerful than Fairtrade/Organic in specialty |
| SCA Q Grade | Professional cupping score ≥80 | Industry signal; use in B2B only |
| Direct Trade | No standard definition — self-declared | Powerful brand story if authentic |

**YourWave recommendation:** Prioritize direct/transparent sourcing story over certifications. Specialty consumers increasingly trust "we visited this farm" over logo certification. Organic where available at acceptable price premium.

## 6. Key Decisions for Phase 2

### Decision 1: First Own Roaster
**Recommendation:** Aillio Bullet R2 as first own machine.
- Reasons: No gas infrastructure needed, best data/software, strong community for learning, reasonable resale value
- Timing: Month 12-18 after launch (when volume warrants it)

### Decision 2: Premises Strategy
**Options:**
A. Home-based roasting (garage/studio) — lowest cost, feasible with electric Bullet
B. Rented commercial kitchen time-share — more professional, €200-400/month
C. Own commercial space — Year 3+ only

**Recommendation:** Home-based Phase 2, commercial space Phase 3

### Decision 3: First Sourcing Relationship
**Recommendation:** Jaká káva for Phase 1 (lowest MOQ, CZ-based logistics)
Then Algrano for Phase 2 flagship origins (direct-trade story, competitive pricing)

### Decision 4: Own Roastery vs. Scaled Contract Roasting
**Breakeven math says:** Own roastery economically justified at 200+ kg/month
**Brand story says:** Own roastery valuable from Day 1 if capacity allows
**Recommendation:** Contract roasting Year 1, Bullet R2 Year 2, Giesen W6 Year 4

|------|-----------|--------|------------|
