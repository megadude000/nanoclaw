## Legal Framework Overview

Running a food storage and packaging operation in the Czech Republic requires compliance at three levels:

1. **Business licensing** — živnostenský úřad (Trade License Office)
2. **Food safety registration** — SZPI (Czech Agriculture and Food Inspection Authority)
3. **Hygiene/premises compliance** — KHS (Krajská hygienická stanice / Regional Public Health Office)

The Czech Republic is an EU member, so EU food law applies directly — primarily **Regulation (EC) No. 852/2004 on food hygiene** and **Regulation (EU) No. 1169/2011 on food labeling**. Czech national law is **Act No. 110/1997 Coll. on Foodstuffs**.

### Key Authorities

| Authority | Czech Name | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **SZPI / CAFIA** | Státní zemědělská a potravinářská inspekce | Food safety, quality, labeling oversight |
| **KHS** | Krajská hygienická stanice | Premises hygiene approval, food handler training |
| **ŽÚ** | Živnostenský úřad | Trade license issuance |
| **ČOI** | Česká obchodní inspekce | Packaging and labeling enforcement |
| **SVS** | Státní veterinární správa | Animal-origin products (not relevant for coffee) |

## Step 1 — Trade License (Živnostenský List)

### What it is
The živnostenský list (trade license / "živno") is the foundational business registration required before any commercial activity in the Czech Republic. It registers you as OSVČ (self-employed) or as a legal entity, and assigns you an IČO (company registration number).

### Type of Trade for Coffee/Food Business

Food businesses fall under **regulated or craft trades (řemeslná živnost)**, not the simple unregulated (volná živnost) category. This means you may need to demonstrate professional qualification, education, or experience in food production.

For **packaging and selling pre-roasted coffee** (not roasting yourself), this is typically classified closer to food retail/wholesale trade (volná or vázaná živnost) rather than production, which is somewhat simpler.

Specific relevant trade categories:
- **Velkoobchod a maloobchod** (wholesale and retail) — for buying and reselling coffee
- **Výroba potravinářských výrobků** (food product manufacturing) — if doing significant processing, portioning, or packaging

### How to Register

1. Visit any živnostenský úřad (Trade Licensing Office) or use Czech Point
2. Submit the Jednotný registrační formulář (Unified Registration Form)
3. Provide: passport/ID, proof of Czech address, criminal record extract, business address proof
4. Pay the administrative fee: **1,000 CZK**
5. Receive confirmation within **5 business days** (IČO activation within 2 working days)

### Key Notes
- The živnostenský úřad forwards notifications to social security (ČSSZ) and health insurance automatically if you use the unified form
- You must separately notify your health insurance provider within **8 days** of starting
- You must notify social security (ČSSZ) within **15 days** of starting

### Cost
- **Trade license fee: 1,000 CZK** (one-time)
- Registration itself is otherwise free

## Step 2 — SZPI Registration (Food Business Operator)

### What it is
Every entity that places food on the market (sells, distributes, stores for commercial purposes) must register with SZPI as a **Food Business Operator (FBO / provozovatel potravinářského podniku)**.

This applies whether you sell:
- In a physical store
- Via an e-shop
- Wholesale B2B
- At markets

### How to Register

Registration is done via the **online form on szpi.gov.cz** — it is free and does not require a physical visit.

When registering, specify your activity type:
- **OI code**: e-shop with physical storage
- **OJ code**: e-shop without storage (dropshipping)
- Include all domain names / websites where you sell

### What SZPI Inspects

SZPI inspectors have authority to:
- Access your premises unannounced
- Take product samples for analysis
- Check documentation (HACCP records, traceability, labeling)
- Inspect your online shop

Non-compliance can result in fines up to **CZK 3,000,000** and mandatory product withdrawal. Persistent or health-endangering violations: up to **CZK 4,000,000**. Additionally, products may be listed on "**Potraviny na pranýři**" (food in the pillory) — the public shame list, which can be devastating for a brand.

### Cost
- **SZPI FBO registration: free**

## Step 3 — KHS Notification (Regional Public Health Authority)

### Who needs this
KHS notification/approval is required for:
- Food service businesses (restaurants, catering, meal delivery)
- Premises where food is prepared or processed
- Any provozovna (business premises) used for food handling

For **pure storage and packaging of shelf-stable packaged goods** (like sealed coffee bags you receive from a roaster), the KHS role is smaller but still relevant — you may need to notify them and meet hygiene requirements for your workspace.

### Process
- Notify the relevant **regional KHS** (the one covering your premises location)
- Submit no later than **the day you commence activities**
- Provide: business details, premises address, description of activity, operating rules (provozní řád)

### The Provozní Řád (Operating Rules Document)

You must prepare and display an operating rules document (provozní řád) covering:
- Layout of the premises and purpose of each room/area
- Hygiene procedures and schedules
- Pest control measures
- Waste management and disposal
- Staff hygiene rules
- Cleaning and disinfection procedures
- How you handle allergens

### Cost
- **KHS notification: free**
- Preparing the provozní řád may cost money if you hire a consultant

## HACCP Requirements

### Legal Basis
HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) is **mandatory under EU Regulation (EC) No. 852/2004** for all food business operators in the Czech Republic. No exceptions for small businesses — though the framework allows for **simplified, flexible implementation** based on your size and complexity.

### What HACCP Means in Practice for Coffee Storage/Packaging

For a micro-business storing and packaging pre-roasted coffee, HACCP is relatively simple because:
- Coffee is a shelf-stable, low-moisture product
- Main hazards are physical (foreign objects), chemical (contamination from cleaning products), and pest-related
- No temperature-sensitive pathogens

Your HACCP plan should cover:
1. **Hazard analysis** — what could go wrong with your coffee (contamination, moisture ingress, pest access)
2. **Critical Control Points** — e.g., checking incoming goods, sealed storage, pest checks
3. **Critical limits** — e.g., storage temperature <25°C, humidity <60%
4. **Monitoring procedures** — temperature/humidity log, visual inspection
5. **Corrective actions** — what to do if a problem is found
6. **Record keeping** — mandatory, must be available for SZPI inspection
7. **Verification** — periodic review of the system

### HACCP Implementation Options

- **DIY**: For micro-businesses, you can create your own simplified HACCP documentation using the Ministry of Agriculture's published good hygiene practice guides
- **Consultant**: Czech HACCP consultants typically charge **5,000–20,000 CZK** for a complete HACCP implementation package
- **Certification bodies**: TÜV Rheinland, LL-C Certification, SGS, DQS operate in the Czech Republic and offer full HACCP certification (optional but adds credibility)
- HACCP certificates are typically **valid for 3 years**, then require recertification

### Good Hygiene Practice (GHP) Guides
The Czech Ministry of Agriculture publishes voluntary guides of good manufacturing and hygiene practice (Příručky správné hygienické praxe) for various food categories — these are publicly available and useful for building your HACCP documentation.

## Physical Space Requirements

### General Requirements for Any Food Business Premises

Under EU Regulation 852/2004 Annex II and Czech hygiene law, any space used for food storage or packaging must:

**Infrastructure:**
- Be kept clean and in good repair
- Have adequate natural or mechanical ventilation
- Have adequate natural or artificial lighting
- Have potable (drinking-quality) water available
- Have adequate sanitary facilities (toilet, hand washing basin with hot/cold water, soap)
- Have proper drainage

**Separation of areas:**
- Separate storage for raw vs. packaged goods
- "Clean" operations (finished goods, packaging) must be separated from "dirty" operations (waste, cleaning)
- Separate staff area with changing facilities

**Surfaces and materials:**
- Floors, walls, ceilings must be smooth, washable, non-toxic
- Work surfaces must be in good condition, smooth, washable, non-absorbent
- Equipment must be cleanable and, where necessary, disinfectable

**Pest control:**
- Adequate pest control measures must be in place
- No pest access to food storage areas

**Waste management:**
- Food waste and other refuse must be deposited in closable containers
- Must have a contract with an authorized waste disposal company (not municipal bins)
- Failure can result in fines up to **CZK 1,000,000**

### Temperature and Humidity for Coffee

Roasted coffee is best stored at:
- **Temperature**: 15–25°C (cool, stable; avoid heat above 25°C)
- **Humidity**: below 60% relative humidity (ideally 50–60%)
- Away from direct sunlight
- Away from strong odors (coffee absorbs aromas)

These parameters are good practice rather than legally prescribed for shelf-stable packaged coffee specifically, but they are relevant when demonstrating HACCP compliance.

### Minimum Viable Physical Setup

For a micro coffee packaging operation, you need at minimum:
- A designated, clean, dry room or space — not shared with food for personal consumption
- A hand washing basin with hot and cold water and soap dispenser
- A mop/cleaning cupboard (úklidová místnost)
- Clearly defined storage area for coffee
- A separate area for packaging operations
- Proper waste bins (food waste, packaging waste, regular waste — separated)
- First aid kit
- Pest control measures (traps, no gaps in walls/floors)

## Coffee Specifically: Roasted Coffee = Processed Food

### Classification
Roasted coffee is classified as a **processed food of plant origin** under Czech and EU food law. This means:

- Full EU food hygiene regulations apply (Reg. 852/2004)
- Labeling must comply with EU 1169/2011
- SZPI is the primary inspection authority (not KHS, which deals more with catering)
- If you import green coffee and roast it, you add a processing step that increases regulatory complexity
- If you buy pre-roasted coffee and repackage into smaller bags, you are a **food packager/retailer** — a simpler category

### Labeling Requirements (Czech & EU Law)
Coffee sold in Czech Republic must be labeled in **Czech language** with:
- Product name
- List of ingredients (for blends)
- Net weight
- Best before date (date of minimum durability — **datum minimální trvanlivosti**)
- Name and address of the food business operator (your company)
- Country of origin (for single-origin coffee)
- Nutritional information (can be exempted for micro-businesses under 10 employees and under 20M CZK annual turnover)
- Any allergen information

### Specific Rules for Coffee Import
If you import green or roasted coffee from outside the EU, you must:
- Notify SZPI under **Decree No. 172/2015 Coll.** upon arrival of each shipment via the SZPI web form
- This applies to first recipients/importers of foodstuffs of plant origin

## Can You Run From Home?

### Short Answer: Yes, but with conditions

Under Czech and EU law, home-based food businesses are permissible but regulated. SZPI explicitly acknowledges the existence of producers who make and sell food from home.

### What Makes It Legal

A home-based food business is legal if:
- It is systematic and continuous (not just occasional)
- You register with SZPI as an FBO
- You comply with EU Reg. 852/2004 hygiene requirements for the part of your home used for food preparation/storage
- The space used for food business is functionally separate from your personal living/cooking area

### What SZPI Checks at Home Premises

SZPI will inspect:
- Hygiene compliance of the workspace (clean, washable surfaces, pest control, etc.)
- HACCP documentation
- Labeling and traceability records
- Storage conditions

Importantly, SZPI has stated that **commissioning approval** (formal building change-of-use permit) is **not essential** for CAFIA inspection purposes — they focus on hygiene compliance, not planning permission.

### What KHS May Require

If your activity is more processing-oriented (repackaging involves opening and handling food), your regional KHS may require:
- A formal notification of your premises
- Operating rules (provozní řád)
- Possible premises inspection

### Practical Reality for Coffee Packaging at Home

For simply storing sealed bags of pre-roasted coffee and repacking into smaller bags:
- Home operation is feasible with low regulatory friction
- The biggest practical issues are space, cleanliness standards, and odor control
- You should NOT do this in a regular kitchen you also use for personal cooking
- A dedicated room, garage, or outbuilding is better
- Getting a dedicated small commercial unit is much cleaner legally and practically

## Registration Process Summary & Costs

### Checklist — Complete Compliance Path

| Step | Action | Authority | Cost | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Obtain trade license (živnost) | Živnostenský úřad | 1,000 CZK | 2–5 business days |
| 2 | Register as FBO | SZPI (online form) | Free | Immediate/same day |
| 3 | Notify public health authority | KHS (regional) | Free | Before starting |
| 4 | Notify trade licensing office of your provozovna | Živnostenský úřad | Free | 3 days before opening |
| 5 | Implement HACCP | Self/consultant | 0–20,000 CZK | Before starting |
| 6 | Prepare provozní řád | Self/consultant | 0–5,000 CZK | Before starting |
| 7 | Register for social security | ČSSZ | Free | Within 15 days |
| 8 | Register for health insurance | Health insurer | Free | Within 8 days |
| 9 | VAT registration (if applicable) | Tax office | Free | When threshold reached |

### Ongoing Monthly Costs (OSVČ, 2025 figures)
- **Social security minimum**: 4,759 CZK/month
- **Health insurance minimum**: 3,143 CZK/month
- **Total minimum**: ~7,902 CZK/month (~315 EUR/month)

### Income Tax
- 15% on income up to CZK 1,762,812/year
- 23% on income above this threshold
- OSVČ can use the 60/40 expense flat-rate: 60% of gross revenue is automatically deductible, only 40% is taxable

### VAT Thresholds (2025)
- Voluntary below CZK 2,000,000 annual turnover
- Mandatory from following year if turnover exceeds CZK 2,000,000
- Immediately mandatory if turnover exceeds CZK 2,536,500 in a calendar year

## Recommended Path for a Micro Coffee Business

### Phase 1 — Home/Garage (0–6 months, zero rent cost)

**If testing/pre-launch:**
1. Designate a specific room/area exclusively for coffee storage and packaging (NOT shared with personal kitchen)
2. Install a utility sink with hot/cold water if not present
3. Create your provozní řád and HACCP documentation (use a template/consultant)
4. Register with SZPI as an FBO (free, online)
5. Notify your regional KHS
6. Get your živnostenský list (1,000 CZK)
7. Ensure proper storage conditions (cool, dry, pest-controlled)

This is legally permissible under Czech/EU law for systematic commercial activity from a private address.

**Monthly overhead**: ~7,900 CZK (social + health insurance minimum) + consumables

### Phase 2 — Dedicated Small Unit (6–18 months)

**Target**: 30–60 sqm unit in Prague or vicinity

**Best immediate options:**
- Check Sreality.cz for units in Praha 9 (Horní Počernice, Vysočany) — excellent transport links, lower prices (200–280 CZK/sqm)
- Check Sklady Praha for their 20–60 sqm temperature-controlled units
- Contact Czech Storage about their Horní Počernice facility

**Target budget**: 8,000–15,000 CZK/month for 30–60 sqm

**What to ensure before signing:**
- Landlord allows food business operation (potravinářství)
- Space has or can have: running water access, proper drainage, ventilation
- Building permit/zoning allows commercial food operation (check with landlord and local building authority)
- You can set up as a proper provozovna (notify ŽÚ 3 days before opening)

### Phase 3 — Scale/Outsource (18+ months)

If order volume justifies it, move to a fulfillment service like DigiPACK or Wellpack. You provide the inventory, they pick/pack/ship. This removes the need for your own warehouse entirely.
