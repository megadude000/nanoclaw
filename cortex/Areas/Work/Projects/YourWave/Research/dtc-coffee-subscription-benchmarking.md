## 1. Company Profiles

### 1.1 Trade Coffee (USA)

**Model:** Multi-roaster marketplace — Trade does not roast. They act as a curated broker connecting subscribers with 50+ independent US specialty roasters. 400+ coffees available.

**Operations:**
- Each bag is roasted to order by the partner roaster, shipped within 24 hours of roasting
- Subscriber receives coffee 3–5 days after roast
- Roasters ship directly from their facility (distributed fulfillment)
- No single warehouse — logistics are handled roaster-side

**Onboarding:**
- Detailed multi-page quiz: brew method, how you drink it (milk? black?), flavor characteristics (fruity vs chocolatey vs nutty), roast preference, bag size, frequency, prepay vs pay-as-you-go
- Quiz feeds a "coffee algorithm" — ongoing rating system refines recommendations
- Users can update taste preferences anytime; affects future "Up Next" suggestions

**Subscription options:**
- "The Hookup" — matchmaking model, algorithm-driven rotation with feedback loop
- "The Classics" — calibration bag → locked-in repeat delivery of confirmed preference
- "Auto-Reorder" — exact beans on a chosen schedule
- Themed subscriptions: Single Origin, Cold Brew, Best Sellers
- Prepaid bundles: 3, 6, or 12 bags

**Packaging:**
- Airtight zip-lock, wide-mouth, lightproof bag — sourced from partner roasters' own packaging
- No standardized Trade box; each roaster ships in their own branded bag

**Pricing:** $15–22/bag; shipping included in subscription plans or $2/bag à la carte; free shipping on $30+ orders

**Flexibility:** Easy pause, skip, cancel; subscription portal self-service

**Gift subscriptions:** Yes — recipient takes quiz to get matched, personalized gift card with message

**Technology:** Shopify-based with Recharge for subscriptions; proprietary matching algorithm; rating and feedback system

**Key differentiator:** Scale + matchmaking. Not replicable for a small brand — but the *quiz-as-onboarding* model is highly instructive.

### 1.2 Nomad Coffee (Barcelona, EU)

**Model:** Self-roasting specialty roastery with physical cafes, wholesale, and DTC subscription.

**Operations:**
- Own Probat UG22 roaster + second roaster at Cal Nomad (Poblenou neighborhood)
- Roasts every week (Monday–Thursday); ships Tuesday–Thursday only
- Processing time: 24–48 hours from order
- Sourcing: small specialty importers worldwide

**Subscription options:**
- "Classics" (year-round selection) from €14/month — 1 bag (250g)
- "Specials" (monthly rotating) from €17/month
- Brewing method selection: Filter / Espresso / Mixed / Decaf
- Quantities: 1 bag (250g), 2, 3, 4 bags, or Big Bag (1kg)
- Grind: whole bean, cold brew, Moccamaster, French Press, AeroPress, V60, Moka, Espresso

**Pricing example:**
- 1 × 250g Classics = €14/month
- 2 × 250g Specials = €25.50/month

**Shipping:**
- Spain/Portugal: free over €30, carrier: Nacex
- EU 27 countries (including CZ, SK, PL, DE, FR etc.): free over €80; carriers: UPS, DHL
- Domestic delivery: 24–48h; EU delivery: 48–96h
- Tracking email sent upon dispatch

**Flexibility:** Full customer portal — skip, pause, change plan, change grind, change address, change payment date

**Onboarding flow:** Variety → brew method → quantity → grind preference → checkout. No quiz; preference-based selection.

**Key differentiator:** Clean EU-wide subscription at accessible price points, own roasting, high flexibility. Strong B2B + DTC hybrid.

### 1.3 Has Bean / Ozone (UK)

**Model:** Pioneer of the UK specialty subscription. Founded 2002 by Steve Leighton in Stafford. Acquired by Ozone Coffee Roasters (NZ) in 2018 — both brands operate independently.

**Operations:**
- Self-roasting from origin; large range of rotating single-origins and blends
- Was the first UK specialty subscription ("In My Mug")
- Weekly dispatch — highest dispatch frequency in specialty (weekly, fortnightly, monthly)
- Roast-to-order model; coffee dispatched shortly after roasting

**Subscription format ("In My Mug"):**
- Rotating selection curated by Steve Leighton — not subscriber-selected
- Weekly dispatch was the original format; now more frequency flexibility
- Named bags with detailed tasting notes; heavy educational component via videos

**What made it distinctive:**
- Steve Leighton's personal video series ("In My Mug") with every shipment — narrated the story of each coffee, roasting decision, origin context. High personal brand equity.
- Tamper Tantrum podcast built community
- Very large range enabled true weekly rotation without repeats

**Packaging:** Branded bags, roast date prominent, detailed tasting card included

**Key differentiator:** Founder as personality — education and entertainment as retention tools. The personal video with each delivery is a model worth studying.

**Current status:** Post-Steve, customer feedback noted loss of personal voice in communications. Lesson: the founder-narrator creates a loyalty flywheel that is hard to replace.

### 1.4 Koppi Roasters (Helsingborg, Sweden)

**Model:** Boutique single-roastery DTC. Founded 2007 by Anne Lunell and Charles Nystrand. Ranked #6 globally among top 100 roasters.

**Operations:**
- Charles roasts; Anne travels to origin building direct producer relationships
- Light Nordic-style roasts for all brewing methods
- DTC via web shop + monthly subscription

**Subscription:**
- 2 or 4 × 250g bags per shipment; filter coffee OR Footprints espresso; whole bean only
- Dispatched last week of each month
- Sign-up before 25th = box that month; after 25th = following month
- Subscribers get first access to special releases and exclusive micro-lots reserved for subscription boxes only

**Shipping:**
- EU: PostNord standard
- Global: PostNord or optional DHL Express (extra fee, full tracking)

**Gift subscriptions:** 3-month gift pack available

**What's included:** No tasting cards mentioned; the coffees themselves are the experience. Koppi's identity is the product.

**Key differentiator:** Micro-lot exclusivity for subscribers — creates scarcity/privilege dynamic. Subscribers feel like insiders.

### 1.5 La Cabra (Aarhus, Denmark)

**Model:** High-end specialty roastery with cafes, bakery, and global subscription. Founded 2012; established in 60+ countries.

**Operations:**
- Self-roasting; "single roast philosophy" — one roast profile per coffee, works for both filter and espresso
- Sources directly from producers; seasonal and micro-lot focus
- Ships every weekday

**Subscription ("Rituals"):**
- Two distinct coffees per month (seasonal exclusives + micro-lots)
- 2 × 250g (one of each) or 4 × 250g (two of each) per shipment
- Occasionally 200g bags of exceptional lots
- Monthly auto-renewal; frequency adjustable

**Pricing:**
- DKK-priced (Denmark primary): ~279 DKK/month (≈€37) for 2 × 250g
- 3-month prepaid: 749 DKK (~€100) vs 837 DKK regular (10% discount)
- 6-month prepaid: 1,429 DKK (~€190) vs 1,674 DKK regular (14.6% discount)
- Gift subscription from 558 DKK (~€74)

**Subscriber perks:**
- 15% off all extra purchases (coffee + equipment) shipped with subscription
- Early access to limited releases
- First delivery includes complimentary Steeped coffee bag

**Gift subscriptions:** Fully digital — recipient receives gift card by email on chosen date; they enter shipping details and start their own subscription. No payment details required from recipient.

**LC Membership:** Separate free program — 10% first order discount, secret sales, factory tour invitations, early access

**Flexibility:** Customer portal for frequency change, quantity change, skip shipment, cancel

**Onboarding:** Not quiz-based — subscription is curated by La Cabra's team. Subscribers join the journey, not configure it.

**Key differentiator:** Premium positioning with luxury subscriber perks. Prepaid discounts drive commitment. Impeccable brand — every touchpoint signals high craft.

## 2. Czech & Slovak Market

### 2.1 Doubleshot (Prague)

**Founded:** 2009/2010. Prague roastery + 3 cafes + training center + bakery. The most respected Czech specialty roastery internationally.

**Subscription:**
- Dispatched first Wednesday of every month
- Bag size: 350g (their only retail format — "best consumed in 2–3 weeks")
- Quantities: 1, 2, or 3 bags per month
- 2+ bags = free Czech shipping
- EU deliveries: DHL International, fees included in price

**Domestic carriers:** PPL, Czech Post, Zásilkovna (subscriber's choice)

**Included with delivery:**
- Newsletter + video blog with each shipment (details on origin, processing, farmer philosophy)
- Decaf option available
- "Add to subscription" window between 23rd–25th of month for extras

**Flexibility:** Pause on vacation, add coffee, add gear to shipment

**Pricing:** Not publicly listed in standard search results — requires direct visit or account

**Key differentiator:** Newsletter + video blog is their Has Bean equivalent. Strongest brand equity in CZ specialty. Hard to outcompete on brand, but their DTC UX is traditional and not discovery-focused.

### 2.2 Los Muertos Coffee (Czech Republic)

**Model:** Specialty coffee subscription, distinctive brand (skull aesthetic, seasonal packaging artwork).

**Subscription:**
- Two editions: Espresso Edition and Filter Edition
- Quantities: 250g, 500g, 1000g per month
- Lengths: 3, 6, or 12 months (commit upfront)
- Orders by 12th = ships 15th of that month
- Pricing: from €39.10–€40.30/month for 250g (expensive for CZ market)
- Free shipping on orders over €60
- Next-day dispatch for paid orders before 6 PM

**What's included:**
- Custom packaging design per shipment (skull motif to seasonal artwork)
- Roast date clearly marked
- Free grinding service in preferred style
- Exclusive/limited batches not available in shop

**Key differentiator:** Strong brand identity + limited edition exclusivity. The "gift a subscription" angle is built in via fixed-term plans.

### 2.3 Inbox Coffee (Prague, founded 2016)

**Model:** Inspired by US subscription model; roast-to-order, single-origin, hand-packed.

**Operations:**
- Fixed 4-week cycle: cut-off Monday, roast Tuesday, ship Wednesday morning
- Free Czech Republic shipping, commitment-free
- 100% satisfaction guarantee
- Sourcing: Africa + South/Central America, seasonal, traceable origin

**What's included:**
- Individual brewing method recommendation per coffee
- Tasting notes in accessible language
- Roaster Lukas personally roasts and recommends

**Key differentiator:** "Roasted for me" personal framing. Cut-off + roast + ship cycle creates freshness ritual. Very small operation — community-scale.

**Key differentiator:** Breadth of selection beats any single roaster in variety.

## 3. Cross-Company Patterns

### Operations Models

| Company | Self-Roasting? | Dispatch Frequency | Batch Size (consumer) |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Trade Coffee | No (marketplace) | Continuous (per order) | 250–340g (varies by roaster) |
| Nomad | Yes | Weekly (Tue–Thu) | 250g |
| Has Bean | Yes | Weekly / Fortnightly / Monthly | Various |
| Koppi | Yes | Monthly (last week) | 250g |
| La Cabra | Yes | Monthly (weekdays) | 250g |
| Doubleshot | Yes | Monthly (1st Wednesday) | 350g |
| Inbox Coffee | Yes | Monthly (4-week cycle) | Not specified |
| Los Muertos | Yes | Monthly (15th) | 250g |

**Pattern:** Monthly dispatch dominates at the small/boutique end. Weekly is operationally intensive — suitable only once volume justifies staffing.

### Freshness Philosophy

All companies studied prioritize **roast-to-order or close-to-roast dispatch**. The window that matters:
- Specialty coffee peak flavor: 5–21 days post-roast
- Roast → pack → ship within 24–48h is the gold standard
- Monthly dispatch works if roast date is timed correctly (roast on dispatch day or day before)

### Box Contents

No company studied ships "just coffee." All include at minimum:
- Printed tasting notes or origin card
- Newsletter (email or physical insert)
- Several include: QR code to brew guide, video content, or detailed origin story

## 4. Operational Best Practices

### Practice 1: Roast-to-Order + Fixed Dispatch Day

**What the leaders do:** Roast coffee within 24–48 hours of dispatch. Fix dispatch day(s) so customers build anticipation. Nomad ships Tue–Thu only; Doubleshot ships first Wednesday of month; Inbox roasts Tuesday, ships Wednesday.

**Why it matters:** Freshness is a promise, not a feature. Customers who receive stale coffee cancel. Customers who experience peak-freshness coffee become evangelists.

**For YourWave:** Coordinate with contract roaster on fixed roast days. Build your dispatch calendar around roast schedule — not the other way around. If roaster can roast on Monday, ship Tuesday. Announce the schedule publicly ("Your Wave ships every Tuesday").

---

### Practice 2: Tiered Quantity + Single Format

**What the leaders do:** One bag format (250g is the global standard, Doubleshot uses 350g) in 1, 2, or 4 quantity options. Avoid SKU complexity at launch.

**Why it matters:** Operational simplicity. One bag size means one packaging run, predictable inventory, easy scaling.

**For YourWave:** Start with 200g (matches your existing spec for drip bags) or 250g (industry standard). Offer 1 or 2 bag options only. Add more later.

---

### Practice 3: Domestic Carriers First, EU via Established Integrators

**What the CZ leaders do:**
- Zásilkovna (Packeta) is the Czech standard for affordable nationwide delivery (~60–100 CZK per shipment to pickup points, cheaper than home delivery)
- PPL and Czech Post for home delivery
- DHL for EU exports

**Pricing structure seen:**
- 1 bag: shipping fee applies
- 2+ bags: free shipping (classic incentive to upsize)
- EU: include shipping in the price (simplicity over itemization)

**For YourWave:** Use Zásilkovna as primary Czech carrier (pickup points = lower cost + no missed deliveries). Home delivery with PPL as premium option. For EU expansion (Phase 2+), integrate DHL or GLS.

---

### Practice 4: Subscription Management via Dedicated Portal

**What the leaders do:** Every company studied offers self-service subscription management — no email to customer service required. Customers can: pause, skip, change frequency, change quantity, update address, add extras.

**Why it matters:** Inability to pause is the #1 cause of cancellation. When life happens, customers need an easy "pause" not a "cancel."

**For YourWave:** Even before building custom platform, use a subscription app (Shopify Subscriptions native app at launch; Recharge when you scale) that provides a self-service portal. Non-negotiable.

---

### Practice 5: Newsletter/Education as Retention Tool

**What the leaders do:**
- Doubleshot: newsletter + video blog with every shipment (origin story, farmer, philosophy)
- Has Bean: weekly video series with personal narration by founder
- La Cabra: seasonal narrative around "Rituals" — themed, story-driven
- Koppi: subscriber-exclusive early access to micro-lot releases

**Why it matters:** Coffee without context is a commodity. Coffee with a story is an experience. Churn drops when customers feel like members of a community, not recipients of packages.

**For YourWave:** Every shipment should include a physical insert card + email. The card: origin, farmer name, altitude, processing method, tasting notes, brew recipe. The email: founder voice, why this coffee was chosen, what makes it unique. QR code on bag links to brew video or origin page.

## 5. Customer Experience Practices

### Practice 1: Onboarding Quiz (or Guided Selection)

**The gold standard (Trade Coffee):** Multi-step quiz covering brew method, flavor preferences, roast level, frequency, bag size. Quiz reduces early churn by 20% because customers feel the product is tailored to them.

**The simpler alternative (Nomad):** Structured selection — variety type, brew method, quantity, grind. No quiz, but guided flow. Faster, still personalized.

**The curated-only model (La Cabra, Koppi):** No customization — you subscribe to the roastery's seasonal selection. Works only when brand equity is high enough that customers trust the curator.

**For YourWave:** At launch, use guided selection (not full quiz — too complex to build). 4 steps: (1) brew method, (2) flavor profile [fruity / chocolatey / balanced], (3) quantity, (4) frequency. Phase 2: add AI-assisted matching.

---

### Practice 2: Pause/Skip/Cancel — Frictionless

**Industry standard:** All studied companies offer frictionless pause and skip. Companies that don't see significantly higher involuntary churn.

**Key design insight from the research:** "Inability to pause is the #1 reason for cancellation." The pause option converts what would be a cancellation into a 4–8 week hiatus, after which many subscribers return.

**For YourWave:** Pause must be 1-click from the subscriber dashboard. Offer "skip next shipment" as a less permanent option. No cancellation friction beyond a single confirmation step.

---

### Practice 3: Gift Subscriptions as a Growth Channel

**How leaders do it:**
- La Cabra: digital gift card sent on chosen date; recipient enters own details; no payment info required
- Trade Coffee: recipient takes quiz to get matched; gifter adds personalized message
- Koppi: 3-month physical gift pack
- Los Muertos: fixed-term plans (3/6/12 months) double naturally as gifts

**Why it matters:** Gift subscriptions have a ~25–30% conversion rate to self-paying subscribers after the gift period ends. They also bring in customers who would never have discovered you organically.

**For YourWave:** Build gift subscription from Day 1. Keep it simple: choose 1, 3, or 6 months, choose quantity, enter recipient email, write a message, choose delivery date. Recipient redeems with their own address. No separate SKU needed.

## 6. Technology Stack

### The Dominant Stack for Small Specialty Roasters

**E-commerce:** Shopify Basic (~€29/month)

**Subscriptions:**
| Tool | Cost | Best For |
|------|------|----------|
| Shopify Subscriptions (native) | Free | Launch — 0 to ~200 subscribers |
| Bold Subscriptions | ~$20–39/month | 200–500 subscribers |
| Recharge | $99+/month + 1% fee | 500+ subscribers, advanced features |

**Email:**
- Klaviyo — free to 500 contacts, then usage-based. Tight Shopify integration. Best for automated flows (welcome, churn prevention, win-back).

**Shipping:**
- Packeta/Zásilkovna API for Czech pickup points
- ShipStation or EasyShip for multi-carrier management (Phase 2)

**Analytics:** GA4 + Shopify built-in at launch

**Customer support:** Gorgias (Shopify-native helpdesk) or Tidio (chat)

**For YourWave (custom platform with Astro + Supabase):**
Your platform replaces Shopify. Subscription management can be built natively in Supabase (recurring charges via Stripe Billing). This is architecturally correct but requires implementing what Recharge gives you out-of-the-box:
- Subscriber portal (pause, skip, change)
- Recurring charge management
- Failed payment retry logic (dunning)
- Subscription analytics

**Recommendation:** Consider using Stripe Billing directly (not Recharge) since you're building custom. Stripe's subscription APIs are well-documented and handle dunning, proration, and trial periods natively.

### QR Codes on Packaging

Use **dynamic QR codes** (via QR.io, Beaconstac, or similar — ~$5–15/month) on every bag. Destination URL can be updated without reprinting. Link to:
- Brew guide specific to this coffee
- Origin story / farm video
- Tasting notes with recommended recipe
- Feedback form ("How did you like this batch?")

Dynamic codes allow you to track scan rates — a proxy for subscriber engagement.

## 7. Unit Economics

### Benchmarks (synthesized from research)

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| DTC gross margin | 60–75% |
| Contract roasting gross margin | 45–60% (lower than self-roasting) |
| Net margin after CAC | 10–20% (if CAC is managed) |
| Profit per 250g bag at €12–15 retail | ~€5–8 |
| Monthly churn rate (coffee subscriptions) | 7–11% (target: <8%) |
| LTV:CAC target ratio | 3:1 minimum |
| Subscription LTV vs one-time purchase | 3–5× higher |
| Acquiring a new customer vs retaining | ~5× more expensive |
| Packaging cost (nitrogen-flushed valve bag) | €0.35–0.45/bag at wholesale volumes |
| Freshness peak window | 5–21 days post-roast |

### YourWave-Specific Estimate (Phase 1, contract roasting)

**Assumptions:**
- Contract roasting: ~€6–8/100g roasted (estimate based on CZ market)
- 250g bag: ~€15–18 roasted cost + packaging (€0.50) + insert card (€0.30) = ~€6–9 COGS
- Retail price: €18–22/bag for specialty single origin
- Gross margin per bag: ~55–65% before shipping
- Zásilkovna shipping (CZ): ~80–120 CZK (~€3.20–4.80) per shipment

**At 100 subscribers (1 bag/month each):**
- Monthly revenue: ~€1,800–2,200
- Monthly COGS: ~€650–900
- Monthly gross profit: ~€1,100–1,400 (~60% gross margin)
- Monthly shipping costs: ~€350–500
- Net contribution before marketing: ~€700–900/month

**Key lever:** Getting subscribers to order 2 bags (free shipping incentive) nearly doubles revenue per subscriber while margin structure improves (shipping cost stays the same).

## 8. What to Avoid

### Mistake 1: No Pause Option

If subscribers cannot pause, they cancel. A pause that takes more than 30 seconds to execute is effectively no pause at all. Build pause-as-a-button, not pause-via-email.

### Mistake 2: Overly Complex SKUs at Launch

Multiple bag sizes, multiple grind options, multiple subscription types — all at launch — creates fulfillment chaos and decision paralysis. Start with: 1 bag size, whole bean or pre-ground (2 grinds max), 1 frequency option (monthly). Add complexity after 50+ subscribers.

### Mistake 3: Batch Roasting for Subscriptions

Roasting a batch once a month and shipping over 2 weeks means the last subscriber to receive their box gets coffee that's already 3+ weeks old. Always dispatch within 48–72h of roasting. Coordinate delivery cutoffs accordingly.

### Mistake 4: Paid Ads Before Organic Validation

Don't spend money on Meta/Google ads until you have at least 20–30 subscribers who found you organically and stayed for 2+ months. Paid ads will amplify a leaky bucket — fix retention first, then scale acquisition.

### Mistake 5: Ignoring Involuntary Churn

Payment failures are responsible for a significant portion of cancellations. Automated dunning (retry logic + email notifications for failed payments) can recover 20–30% of would-be churned subscribers. This must be built into your subscription platform.

### Mistake 6: No Feedback Loop

Without subscriber feedback (ratings, short surveys, cancel reason), you fly blind. Implement a simple post-delivery rating (1–5 stars + comment) from Day 1. Use cancel-reason surveys. This data directly informs sourcing decisions.

### Mistake 7: Underestimating Fulfillment Time

Packing, labeling, printing, carrier pickup — all take longer than expected. A 100-subscriber monthly dispatch can take a full day if done manually. Build your operations model before you need it; don't improvise at dispatch time.

### Mistake 8: Skipping the Roast Date on Packaging

Every specialty coffee consumer checks the roast date. If it's not there, they assume the coffee is old. Roast date is non-negotiable on every bag.

### Mistake 9: Over-investing in Packaging Before Validating Product

One case study (Kloo Coffee) found that customers cared more about taste and efficiency than premium packaging. They switched from premium packaging to recyclable refill pouches — cheaper and more eco-friendly. Don't over-design packaging for launch; validate the coffee first.

### Mistake 10: Scaling Before the Unit Economics Work

A 3:1 LTV:CAC ratio is the minimum for sustainable growth. If you're acquiring customers at €30 CAC and their LTV is €60, you're treading water. Fix retention (→ LTV) and reduce acquisition costs before increasing ad spend.

## 9. Positioning vs Czech Market

### Current Czech Market Landscape

| Player | Strength | Weakness (from YW perspective) |
|--------|----------|-------------------------------|
| Doubleshot | Strongest CZ brand, international recognition | Czech-only language focus, no discovery model, not English-first |
| Inbox Coffee | Roast-to-order, personal feel | Very small, no English interface, Czech-only |
| Los Muertos | Bold branding, exclusive batches | Expensive (€39+/month), fixed-term only |
| Kofio | Widest selection, European roasters | Marketplace, not subscription, no community |
| Mama Coffee | Cafes + retail | No DTC subscription identified |

### YourWave's Competitive Opening

**Gap identified:** English-first DTC specialty coffee subscription with a discovery model in the Czech Republic. No player currently occupies this position.

Prague's 354,000 foreign residents (1 in 4 inhabitants) represent an underserved, high-purchasing-power market that:
- Consumes specialty coffee at higher rates than the general population
- Cannot engage fluently with Czech-language brands
- Is accustomed to DTC subscription models from UK/US/Nordic home markets

**YourWave positioning:** "The coffee subscription for Prague's international community — and specialty coffee explorers everywhere."

**Key differentiations to build:**
1. English-first (then CZ second) — not a translation, a primary language
2. Discovery model ("Explorer Wave" product line) — rotating origins with educational inserts
3. Bundle Builder as core UX — unique in CZ market
4. Founder-voice communications (following Has Bean / Inbox Coffee model)
5. QR code to digital origin story + brew guide on every bag

**What NOT to compete on:**
- Do not try to out-brand Doubleshot on Czech identity — you can't and don't need to
- Do not compete on price with Inbox Coffee — compete on experience
- Do not try to match Kofio on selection — compete on curation and education

## 10. Realistic Timeline: 0 to 100 Subscribers

### Phase 0: Pre-launch Foundation (Months 1–3)

**Goal:** Build audience before selling anything.

Actions:
- Launch @yourwave.coffee Instagram NOW (not when the shop is ready)
- Post 3–4x/week: origin education, behind-the-scenes with contract roaster, coffee culture in Prague
- Start email waitlist immediately — even a simple landing page with Mailchimp form
- Join Prague expat Facebook groups, expat Slack communities, coffee subreddits
- DM 10–20 specialty coffee enthusiasts in Prague per week — genuinely engage, don't pitch
- Commission 2–3 sample batches from contract roaster — photograph, film, taste

**Realistic waitlist target:** 50–150 email subscribers before launch

---

### Phase 1: Soft Launch (Month 4–5)

**Goal:** First 20 paying subscribers.

Actions:
- Email waitlist with launch offer: first month at 20% discount for founding subscribers
- Founding subscriber badge/perks (permanent discount, input on future origins)
- Personal outreach to 20–30 Prague coffee enthusiasts who engaged with you during Phase 0
- Gift 5 subscriptions to local coffee influencers (Prague specialty coffee scene), food bloggers, expat community managers
- No paid ads yet

**Expected conversion:** 10–15% of engaged waitlist → 15–25 paying subscribers in first month

---

### Phase 2: Growth (Months 6–9)

**Goal:** 20 → 100 subscribers.

Actions:
- Referral program: "Give a friend €5, get €5" — implemented in platform
- Gift subscriptions available for sale (Christmas/Easter timing is valuable)
- First 3 months of subscriber feedback incorporated into sourcing
- Start collaborations: guest selection with a local barista or roaster ("Guest Wave")
- Instagram Reels showing unboxing, brew process, origin maps
- Consider 1–2 pop-up events in Prague expat venues (yoga studios, coworking spaces, international cafes)

**Growth rate expectation:** 15–25 new subscribers/month at this stage (mostly organic + referral)

**Expected timeline to 100 subscribers:** 8–12 months from launch

**Caveat:** If a strong gifting push at Christmas (Month 7–8) lands, 100 can be reached faster — gift subscriptions have driven disproportionate growth for early-stage brands.

---

### Churn Management (Ongoing from Month 1)

- Monthly churn target: <8%
- At 100 subscribers with 8% monthly churn: losing ~8 subscribers/month, need 8+ new to maintain
- At 5% monthly churn: only losing 5, much easier to grow
- Key actions: pause option (reduces forced cancellation), monthly feedback email, origin education as engagement, skip option during summer/holiday

## 11. YourWave Synthesis

### Top 5 Operational Practices to Adopt

**1. Fixed Dispatch Day + 48h Roast-to-Ship Window**
Choose one day per week (suggest Tuesday) as dispatch day. Coordinate with contract roaster to roast Monday. Ship Tuesday morning via Zásilkovna. Announce this schedule publicly — it becomes a ritual for subscribers.

**2. Zásilkovna as Primary Czech Carrier + DHL for EU**
Zásilkovna pickup points: lower cost, no missed deliveries, nationwide coverage. Offer home delivery as premium option. When EU shipping begins, use DHL (well-integrated, Nomad's choice for EU).

**3. Subscriber Self-Service Portal (Pause/Skip/Edit)**
Non-negotiable from Day 1. In Supabase-based platform: implement via Stripe Billing portal. Pause = suspend next N billing cycles. Skip = one-time delivery skip. No email to cancel — self-service only.

**4. Single Bag Size + 2-Tier Quantity**
Launch with 250g bag only. 1 bag or 2 bags per shipment. Free shipping at 2 bags. Expand to additional quantities after 50 subscribers.

**5. Roast Date + Dynamic QR Code on Every Bag**
Roast date printed or stamped on every bag. QR code links to origin story + brew guide page (update content per batch without reprinting). This is your digital touchpoint on every physical product.

---

### Top 3 Customer Experience Practices to Adopt

**1. Guided Selection Onboarding (4-step, not a quiz)**
At checkout: (1) Brew method [filter / espresso / both], (2) Flavor profile [fruity & floral / chocolatey & nutty / balanced], (3) Quantity [1 or 2 bags], (4) Frequency [monthly]. Store preferences in subscriber profile. Use them when curating their next batch recommendation.

**2. Founder-Voice Insert Card + Email**
Every shipment: A5 printed card with origin name, country, altitude, processing method, tasting notes, brew recipe, and a 2–3 sentence personal note from the founder ("Why I chose this coffee this month"). Same message via email on dispatch day. This is what Has Bean built — it creates a parasocial relationship between subscriber and brand.

**3. Gift Subscriptions from Day 1**
Simple gifting flow: choose duration (1/3/6 months), quantity, write message, choose delivery date, pay. Recipient gets email with personal message + link to activate. They enter shipping details and preferences. Zero friction for the recipient. Gift subscriptions historically arrive in spikes around Christmas, Valentine's Day, and birthdays — build the feature, then promote it seasonally.

### What to Avoid

1. **No pause option** — biggest churn driver; build it before launch
2. **Complex SKU structure at launch** — simplify to survive operationally
3. **Batch roasting for subscriptions** — always roast within 48h of dispatch
4. **Paid ads before organic validation** — fix product-market fit first
5. **Ignoring involuntary churn** — build dunning/retry logic from Day 1
6. **No feedback mechanism** — post-delivery rating + cancel-reason survey from the start
7. **Over-designing packaging before product validation** — taste beats aesthetics early on
8. **Scaling before 3:1 LTV:CAC** — a leaky bucket that paid ads won't fix
