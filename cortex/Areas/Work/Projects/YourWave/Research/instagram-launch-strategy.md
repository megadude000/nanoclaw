## 1. Pre-Launch Setup

### Account Handle

Secure `@yourwave.coffee` first. If taken, acceptable alternatives: `@yourwave_coffee` or `@yourwavecoffee`. The handle should be memorable and consistent with the domain (`atlas.yourwave.uk`).

---

### Bio Formula

The Instagram bio has 150 characters. Every character earns its place.

**Formula:** [What you are] · [Unique angle] · [CTA with link]

**Draft bio:**
```
Specialty coffee discovery from Prague ☕
English-first. Every bag has a story.
→ 62 coffee guides at atlas.yourwave.uk
```

**Alternative (shorter, more punchy):**
```
Prague-based coffee subscription 🌊
Discover where your coffee comes from.
atlas.yourwave.uk ↓
```

**Why this works:**
- "English-first" immediately signals the positioning differentiator — no Czech competitor owns this
- "Discovery" frames the subscription as exploration, not consumption
- The Atlas CTA does double duty: drives traffic to the content hub and signals credibility (62 articles = we know coffee)
- Emoji used sparingly and purposefully (wave/coffee align with brand name)

**Avoid:** Generic phrases like "premium coffee," "artisan roasts," "quality beans" — meaningless in the specialty coffee space.

---

### Profile Photo

**Recommendation: Brand mark / logomark, not a photo.**

Rationale: At 110×110px (displayed size), anything complex disappears. A strong logomark — the wave motif, clean on a warm cream or deep navy background — is instantly recognisable at small size and scales to all uses (app icon, packaging, etc.).

**If no logomark exists yet:** Use a close-up product photo with strong contrast — a single coffee bag with the wave visible, or a distinctive flat lay with the brand palette. Temporary until the logo is finalised.

**What to avoid:** Founder portrait at launch (this is a brand account, not a personal account). Group photos. Busy images with text.

---

### Highlight Covers

Five Highlights, each with a custom cover icon. Covers should use the brand palette (whichever direction is chosen — see grid aesthetic below). Design them as a set — same style, same proportions, same aesthetic treatment.

| # | Highlight Name | Icon Concept | What Goes Inside |
|---|---------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **About** | Wave icon or compass rose | Brand story, team intro (Andriy + Sha), the "why" behind YourWave, Prague roots |
| 2 | **Atlas** | Open book or map pin | Teasers and links to Atlas articles — origins, processing methods, brew guides |
| 3 | **Coffee School** | Coffee cup with graduation cap or simple bean | Brew tips, method explainers, quick tutorials — educational content |
| 4 | **Roasters** | Flame icon or roaster silhouette | Featured roaster profiles, origin partners, behind-the-scenes from partner roasteries |
| 5 | **Subscribe** | Subscription box or wave arrow | How the subscription works, what's in the box, FAQs, unboxing clips |

**Technical spec:** Highlight covers should be 1080×1920px, saved as JPG. The icon/illustration should sit centred in approximately the middle 40% of the frame — Instagram crops to a circle.

### Link-in-Bio Setup

**Recommendation: Beacons (free tier to start, Creator Pro at $10/month when ready)**

Reasoning:

- **Beacons** offers native analytics, AI-powered post content, zero transaction fees on Pro, and a much cleaner design than Linktree — best fit for YourWave's stage

**Beacons page structure:**
```

```

**Custom domain option:** When ready, point `links.yourwave.uk` or `go.yourwave.uk` to the Beacons page for a seamless brand experience.

### Grid Aesthetic Direction

**Which of the 3 brand directions works best for Instagram?**

The three options (Minimal Wave navy/copper, Terroir-Craft earthy, Bold Czech) map to different Instagram contexts:

| Direction | Instagram Strength | Risk |
|-----------|-------------------|------|
| **Minimal Wave** (navy/copper) | Premium, distinctive, stands out from warm-earthy competitors | Can feel cold or corporate if executed poorly |
| **Terroir-Craft** (earthy) | Aligns with global specialty coffee aesthetic, familiar to the audience | Looks like every other specialty coffee brand — blends in |
| **Bold Czech** | Unique differentiation, memorable, conversation-starting | Requires confident execution; harder to maintain consistency |

**Recommendation: Minimal Wave with warm moments.**

Here is why: Instagram's specialty coffee space (La Cabra, Nomad, September, Workshop) is saturated with earthy cream-and-terracotta aesthetics. Terroir-Craft would position YourWave as "another specialty coffee brand" visually. Bold Czech is too niche and risks alienating the English-first expat audience at launch.

Minimal Wave (deep navy, warm copper/gold accents, cream base) creates:
- Immediate visual recognition when scrolling
- Premium signal that justifies subscription pricing
- Clear differentiation from every Czech competitor
- Works beautifully for Atlas article cover designs

**Execution rules for the grid:**
- **Background consistency:** 80% of posts use the same 2–3 background textures (raw linen, pale grey concrete, warm cream paper)
- **Editing preset:** One Lightroom preset applied to all photos. Slightly lifted shadows, cooled highlights, warm mid-tones. Apply it even to phone photos.
- **Post spacing:** Never post two visually similar posts back-to-back. Alternate: flat lay → text graphic → lifestyle shot → carousel
- **Text graphics:** When text appears on-image, use the brand typeface only. No system fonts.
- **Aspect ratio:** 4:5 portrait (1080×1350px) for feed posts. This takes up more screen real estate in the feed.

## 3. Atlas ↔ Instagram Integration

The Coffee Atlas is YourWave's strongest differentiator. 62 articles in three languages is a content moat no competitor can replicate quickly. The challenge is connecting the two platforms in a way that flows naturally rather than feeling like a content promotion machine.

### The Funnel Model

```
Instagram (discovery + inspiration)
         ↓
Atlas (depth + trust + education)
         ↓
Email capture (owned audience)
         ↓
Subscription (conversion)
```

Each step earns the next. Instagram doesn't sell subscriptions directly — it earns the click to the Atlas. The Atlas doesn't sell subscriptions directly — it earns the email. Email converts.

---

### Turning Atlas Articles Into Instagram Content

Every Atlas article contains multiple Instagram posts. A single 1,500-word article on Ethiopian Yirgacheffe contains:

| Atlas content | Instagram format | Estimated saves |
|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 5 key facts about the origin | Carousel (6 slides) | High |
| "What does washed processing mean" section | Single explanatory graphic | Medium |
| Flavour note breakdown | Reel (sensory description as visual/audio) | High |
| Farm/producer details | Origin story single image with caption | Medium |
| Brew recipe included | Carousel or Reel | Very high |

**Process for Sha:** When Andriy publishes a new Atlas article → Sha reads it and extracts 3–4 Instagram content pieces. Each one takes 20–30 minutes to design and write. One article becomes one week of Instagram content.

**Caption formula for Atlas-to-Instagram:**
1. Hook (first line, stops the scroll)
2. The point (2–3 sentences of actual value)
3. Expand/invite (tease the Atlas article)
4. CTA ("Full guide at atlas.yourwave.uk/[article]")

---

### QR Codes on Packaging → Atlas Article

This is the highest-leverage Atlas integration.

**Implementation:**
- Every coffee bag gets a unique QR code printed on the back (or on the insert card)
- The QR code links to: `atlas.yourwave.uk/coffees/[lot-name]`
- That Atlas page contains: origin story, farm info, processing method, flavour notes, and a brew guide specific to this coffee

**Why this works:**
- Creates a direct physical-to-digital connection that no Czech competitor offers
- The subscriber scans the code with their bag in hand — maximum context, maximum attention
- Atlas visit is guaranteed (no cold traffic); subscriber is already invested in this specific coffee
- After reading the article, the subscriber discovers other Atlas content → more time on site → email capture opportunity

**Instagram angle:** Feature the QR code prominently in post 7 (product reveal) and in Stories when shipping goes live. Show the scan happening. Film a 15-second Reel: hand holds bag, scans QR, Atlas article appears on phone. That moment = the brand promise made visual.

---

### Story Series: "Atlas Deep Dive"

Weekly recurring Story series (every Thursday, for example):

- Slide 1: "This week's Atlas article 📖" + article title
- Slide 2: Key fact or pull quote from the article
- Slide 3: Poll — "Did you know [fact from article]? Yes / No"
- Slide 4: Link sticker — "Read the full guide →"

**Results:** Polls boost the Story's position in the algorithm (the poll interaction counts as engagement). The link sticker in slide 4 benefits from the warm-up — viewers who answered the poll are more likely to click through.

---

### Instagram as SEO Driver for Atlas

Instagram captions now function as search signals on the platform — keywords in captions help Instagram surface content in search. But the Atlas SEO relationship runs in the other direction too:

- Instagram Reels and posts can be embedded in Atlas articles (increases time-on-page for SEO)
- Atlas articles rank for long-tail terms ("how to brew V60," "Ethiopia Yirgacheffe flavour notes") → Google traffic → people find Atlas → follow YourWave Instagram → subscribe

This is a compound flywheel. Start building it from day one by cross-linking everything.

## 4. Hashtag Strategy

### The 2026 Hashtag Reality

Instagram removed the ability to follow hashtags in December 2024. Hashtags now serve one primary function: **helping the algorithm understand what your content is about** so it surfaces in search results and recommends to relevant users.

This changes strategy significantly:
- The old approach (30 hashtags crammed in) is dead
- Quality and relevance matter more than volume
- 5–10 highly relevant tags outperform 25–30 generic tags
- Location-based tags remain strong for local discovery

---

### Tier System

**Tier 1 — Mega tags (1M+ posts):** Use sparingly (1–2 per post maximum). These are brand signals more than discovery tools.

**Tier 2 — Niche tags (50K–500K posts):** Your primary discovery layer. Most of your posts should include 3–4 of these.

**Tier 3 — Micro tags (<10K posts):** Community tags. Lower reach but highest engagement rate — these audiences are dedicated enthusiasts. Use 1–2 per post.

**Tier 4 — Branded tag:** `#YourWave` or `#YourWaveCoffee` — use on every post. Builds a searchable archive of your content.

After 90 days, audit which hashtag sets drove the most profile visits (visible in Instagram Insights) and double down on those.

## 5. Content Calendar Template

### Frequency Recommendation for Launch Phase

**Months 1–3 (Launch Phase):**
- Feed posts: 4–5 per week
- Stories: Daily (3–7 Stories per day)
- Reels: 3 per week (primary growth engine)

This is ambitious for a 2-person team. See the responsibility split below for how this actually works.

**Months 4–6 (Growth Phase):**
- Feed posts: 3–4 per week (quality increases as quantity holds)
- Stories: Daily
- Reels: 3–4 per week

**After 6 months:** Use Instagram Insights to identify your 2 best posting days and 2 best times — then optimise the schedule around actual audience data, not generic advice.

---

### Responsibility Split: Andriy vs. Sha

| Task | Owner | Frequency |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Atlas article publication | Andriy | 2x per week (already happening) |
| Atlas → Instagram content extraction | Sha | Weekly (batch: 1 article → 3–4 posts) |
| Reel filming and editing | Sha (primary), Andriy (when demo needed) | 3x per week |
| Flat lay photography | Sha | Weekly batch session (2 hours, 8–10 shots) |
| Caption writing | Sha | Daily |
| Story creation | Sha | Daily |
| Hashtag research and rotation | Sha | Monthly review |
| Analytics review | Andriy (data) + Sha (content decisions) | Weekly |
| Community management (replies, DMs) | Both | Daily — 20 min each |
| Influencer/collab outreach | Andriy | Monthly |

**Key principle:** Sha should batch-create content, not create daily. One 2-hour photography session per week produces enough visual content for 5–6 posts. Captions can be written in a batched 1–2 hour session. Scheduling tools (Later, Buffer, or Meta's native scheduler) post automatically.

---

### Content Pillars

| Pillar | % of Posts | Purpose | Formats |
|--------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Education** | 40% | Build expertise, earn saves | Carousels, Reels, single graphics |
| **Inspiration** | 25% | Sell the feeling, grow desire | Origin photos, lifestyle imagery, sensory captions |
| **Behind the Scenes** | 20% | Build trust, humanise the brand | Team photos, Stories, process Reels |
| **Product / Commercial** | 15% | Convert to subscribers | Product shots, box reveals, subscription CTAs |

**Rule: Never let Commercial exceed 20%.** If people come to your feed and see 4 out of 5 posts are "subscribe now," they leave. The value needs to dramatically outweigh the ask.

---

### Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Feed Post | Stories | Notes |
|-----|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Monday** | Educational carousel | 3–4 Stories: week preview, poll | Peak engagement day |
| **Tuesday** | Reel (brew guide or origin) | 2–3 Stories: Reel share + link to Atlas | Post Reel 7–9am or 6–8pm |

| **Sunday** | — (or schedule a carousel for Monday am) | 1–2 Stories: quiet, morning ritual vibe | Rest day |

**Total per week:** 4 feed posts + 1 Reel + daily Stories. Achievable for a 2-person team.

---

### Monthly Batch Sessions

**Week 1 of each month:**
- Photography batch (Sha): 2 hours → 8–12 photos for the month
- Content planning (Both): 30 min → map Atlas articles to Instagram content for the month
- Hashtag set update (Sha): 20 min → swap out underperforming tags from previous month

**Week 2:** Execute and publish. Monitor early engagement on first posts.

**Week 3:** Mid-month analytics check. Double down on best-performing content type.

**Week 4:** Plan next month's Atlas → Instagram connections. Draft subscription-focused content for the upcoming push.

## 6. Conversion Tactics

### Story Polls → DM Funnel → Subscription Signup

**The sequence:**

```
Story poll (qualifies the lead)
         ↓
Auto-DM via ManyChat (delivers value + soft CTA)
         ↓
Human follow-up DM (personal, closes)
         ↓
Link to subscription page
```

**Example flow:**

*Story: "Do you currently have a coffee subscription?"*
- Option A: "Yes, but it's not exciting me"
- Option B: "No, but I'm curious"

*ManyChat auto-DM to Option A voters:*

*ManyChat auto-DM to Option B voters:*
> "Specialty coffee subscriptions used to be boring. We're changing that. Every month is a different coffee with a different story — and you get a QR code that takes you to the full origin guide. Our waitlist is open → [link]"

**Why this is powerful:** Instagram DMs have ~90% open rates vs ~20% for email. The poll creates a consent-based entry point — the person voted, so the DM feels relevant, not spam.

### Waitlist CTA Mechanics

Before launch, every conversion goal is the waitlist — not the subscription itself.

**Waitlist mechanics:**

1. **Story swipe (link sticker):** "Join the waitlist — limited spots" → yourwave.uk/waitlist
2. **Comment keyword automation (ManyChat):** Post a feed post with caption "Comment WAVE below and I'll DM you the waitlist link." ManyChat sends the link automatically to everyone who comments. This boosts the comment count (algorithm signal) and builds the DM list simultaneously.
3. **Story countdown sticker:** Set a countdown to launch day. Viewers can subscribe to get notified when the countdown hits zero — free reminder notification from Instagram.
4. **Highlight the waitlist in bio:** When the waitlist is live, the Beacons link bio CTA should lead with it: "→ Join the waitlist (opens [date])"

**Waitlist email capture strategy:** The waitlist page should capture email, not just a name. Email owned channel beats Instagram algorithm forever. Offer something in exchange: "Join the waitlist and get our free PDF: The 10 Coffees You Should Try Before You Die." Atlas content powers this instantly.

### Story Link Sticker Best Practices

Link stickers replace the old "swipe up" and are available to all accounts now (no 10K follower requirement).

**What converts:**
- **Context before the sticker:** Never put a link sticker on the first Story slide. Warm the viewer up first (1–2 slides of value/context), THEN show the sticker in slide 3.
- **Visual framing:** Draw an arrow pointing to the sticker. Sounds obvious — it doubles click rate.
- **Verbal CTA:** Add text next to the sticker. "Tap here →" or "Read the full guide ↗" — even with a visible sticker, the explicit instruction matters.
- **Sticker label customisation:** Rename the sticker text. Instead of the URL, show "Read at the Atlas" or "Get the guide" — much more compelling than `atlas.yourwave.uk/ethiopia...`

**What hurts conversion:**
- Link sticker in the first Story slide (no warm-up = no trust = no click)
- Too many Stories with link stickers (dilutes impact — use them for content that genuinely merits a click)
- No explanation of what the link leads to

### Follower Milestones

| Milestone | Target timeline | What drives it |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| **100 followers** | Week 1–2 | Personal networks (Andriy + Sha share with contacts), first 9 posts live, Reels start circulating |

**Important context:** Follower count is a lagging indicator at this stage. Engagement rate and DM conversations are more meaningful. A 500-follower account with 6% engagement and 50 active DMs is more valuable than a 2,000-follower account with 0.5% engagement and no conversations.

For specialty niche accounts under 10K followers, **4–6% engagement rate is healthy**. The platform median is 0.5% — niche audiences engage far above average.

### When to Start Paid Promotion

**Rule: Do not pay to promote until you have proof of what works organically.**

Paying to amplify content that doesn't resonate organically wastes the budget. The algorithm is telling you something.

**Timeline:**

| Phase | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **Month 1–2** | 100% organic. Build baseline. Let the algorithm learn your content. |
| **Month 3** | Identify your top 2–3 performing posts (by saves + reach). These become your first boosted posts. Budget: €5–10/day for 5–7 days each. |
| **Month 3–4** | If boosts show positive ROAS (profile visits → link clicks → subscriptions), graduate to Meta Ads Manager. Create a proper awareness campaign targeting: Czech Republic + Slovakia + Austria, interests: specialty coffee, home brewing, food & drink subscription. |
| **Month 4–6** | Introduce retargeting: custom audience of Instagram profile visitors in last 30 days. Budget: €15–20/day. |
| **Month 6+** | Lookalike audiences based on email subscribers. This is where real subscriber acquisition efficiency begins. |

**Starting budget recommendation:** €150–200/month for paid amplification from Month 3. This is experimental budget — the goal is learning, not immediate ROI.

**What to boost first:**
1. Your best-performing Atlas carousel (drives traffic to content hub, builds email list)
2. A subscription-focused product post (direct conversion test)
3. A Reel with strong organic watch time already (algorithm will reward boosted content that already performs)

**What not to boost:** Your weakest posts. A post with low organic engagement will perform worse with paid traffic, not better.

### Quick-Win Actions for Week 1

Before the first post goes live:

1. **Follow 50–100 relevant accounts:** Czech coffee shops, global specialty roasters, coffee educators, Prague expat accounts, food/lifestyle accounts in CZ. This seeds the algorithm with YourWave's interest graph.

2. **Leave 10 genuine comments per day** on posts from accounts in your target audience. Not "great post!" — actual engagement: "We've been working with a washed Ethiopian from Gedeo zone too — the bergamot notes are incredible." This builds visibility in those communities before you have followers.

3. **DM 10 personal contacts** (not mass DM) — people Andriy and Sha actually know who might be interested. Tell them what you're building, share the profile, ask for their honest first impression. Early followers from your network signal to the algorithm that your content is resonating.

4. **Tag your location** on every Prague-related post. Instagram location-based discovery is still active and underused by small brands.

5. **Post your first Reel on day 1, not day 5.** Reels are the fastest path to reaching non-followers. Your first 9 grid posts matter, but the Reel is what will grow the account fastest.

Follower count is the public-facing metric. Email subscribers are the real scoreboard.
