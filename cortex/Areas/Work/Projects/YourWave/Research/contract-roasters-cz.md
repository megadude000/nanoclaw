# Contract / Toll Roasting Options in Czech Republic

*Research compiled: 2026-03-23*
*Status: Initial deep research*

---

## Executive Summary

The Czech Republic has a vibrant specialty coffee scene with ~59 roasters nationwide (20 in Prague, 14 in South Moravia). However, **dedicated contract/toll roasting services are not widely advertised** by Czech roasters. Most operate under their own brands with wholesale programs. The best candidates for contract roasting partnerships are roasters with wholesale infrastructure, B2B experience, and sufficient capacity.

**Top candidates for contract roasting inquiry (ranked by likelihood):**
1. **Mamacoffee** (Prague) — explicitly mentions "Private brands" on their site
2. **Doubleshot** (Prague) — wholesale is their main business, large capacity (Probat UG 22)
3. **The Miners** (Prague) — massive B2B infrastructure, 40+ partners, franchise model
4. **Industra Coffee** (Brno) — experienced team, Diedrich IR-12, wholesale program

| Component | Estimated Range (EUR/kg) |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Green coffee (commodity arabica) | 6-8 EUR/kg |
| Green coffee (specialty 85+) | 10-15 EUR/kg |
| Roasting service fee | 3-5 EUR/kg |
| Packaging (basic) | 0.50-1.50 EUR/kg |
| **Total (commodity)** | **9.50-14.50 EUR/kg** |
| **Total (specialty)** | **13.50-21.50 EUR/kg** |

*Note: Czech prices may be 10-20% lower than Western EU due to lower operating costs. No excise duty on coffee in Czech Republic.*

- Greece: 3.00 EUR/kg

## Key Insights & Recommendations

1. **Mamacoffee is the clear frontrunner** — the only Czech roaster found that explicitly offers private brand services, with Fairtrade and organic certifications, and ~$9.5M revenue indicating capacity.

2. **No Czech roaster explicitly advertises "toll roasting"** as a service category like UK/US roasters do. This is common in Central Europe where the concept is less formalized. Direct outreach is essential.

3. **Czech Republic has no coffee excise duty**, making it more competitive than Germany (+2.19 EUR/kg) or Greece (+3.00 EUR/kg) for roasting operations.

4. **Green coffee prices are volatile** — arabica futures have dropped from 2025 highs ($4.40/lb) to ~$3.00/lb but remain elevated vs. historical norms.

5. **Consider multi-roaster approach** — start with 2-3 roasters for sample roasts to compare profiles and service quality before committing.
